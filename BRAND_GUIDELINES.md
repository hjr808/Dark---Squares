## BRAND IDENTITY OVERVIEW

Dark Squares is positioned as a sophisticated urban drama with gothic sensibilities. The visual brand reflects authenticity, consequence, and the moral complexity of urban life. Every design element serves the narrative of choices and their shadows.

## PRIMARY VISUAL LANGUAGE

### Color Palette

#### Primary Colors
| Color | Hex Code | Usage | Purpose |
|-------|----------|-------|---------|
| Deep Black | #0A0A0A | Backgrounds, primary text, shadows | Represents darkness, consequence |
| Metallic Silver | #C0C0C0 | Accents, typography, highlights | Reflects light, hope, contrast |
| Midnight Blue | #1A2B4A | Secondary accents, atmospheric tones | Urban night, depth |
| Gun Metal Gray | #3D3D3D | Secondary text, borders | Neutral professional base |

#### Atmospheric Colors (Photography/Video)
- **Night Sequences:** Deep blacks with selective moonlight/streetlight
- **Urban Scenes:** Desaturated, realistic color palette with metallic highlights
- **Character Lighting:** Dramatic side/rim lighting creating visual depth
- **Mood Grading:** Cool tones with occasional warm street/neon accents

### Typography

#### Primary Font Family
- **Font Name:** Helvetica (per PDF specifications)
- **Weight Hierarchy:**
  - **Bold (H1/Titles):** Helvetica-Bold
  - **Regular (Body/Captions):** Helvetica
- **Usage:**
  - Headlines: Bold, all caps, letter-spaced
  - Body Text: Regular, sentence case
  - Taglines: Bold, italic variations for emphasis

#### Typography Applications

| Element | Font | Weight | Size | Case | Purpose |
|---------|------|--------|------|------|---------|
| Main Title | Helvetica | Bold | 72pt+ | ALL CAPS | Maximum impact |
| Taglines | Helvetica | Bold | 18-24pt | Title Case | Narrative messaging |
| Cast Names | Helvetica | Regular | 14-16pt | Proper Case | Character identification |
| Credits/Info | Helvetica | Regular | 10-12pt | Proper Case | Production details |
| Subtitles | Helvetica | Regular | 12-14pt | Sentence | Context/description |

#### Letter Spacing
- Main titles: +3 to +5 points (expanded for gravitas)
- Taglines: +2 to +3 points (elevated sophistication)
- Body text: Normal spacing (readability)

### Logo & Wordmark

#### Primary Logo
- **Style:** Contemporary serif with metallic treatment
- **Configuration:** "DARK SQUARES" in stacked or linear arrangement
- **Color:** Metallic silver on black background
- **Specifications:**
  - Minimum Size: 2 inches (at 300 DPI)
  - Clear Space: 0.25 inches minimum around logo
  - Background: Black or dark gradient preferred

#### Logo Variations
1. **Full Logo:** Wordmark with subtitle/tagline
2. **Lockup:** Logo with series branding
3. **Icon Version:** Abbreviated mark for social/digital
4. **Monochrome:** Black and white versions for print

#### Brand Mark Applications
- **Primary Use:** Poster headers, opening sequences, digital header
- **Secondary Use:** Social media profiles, digital banners
- **Restricted Use:** Not to be distorted, colored differently, or integrated with unrelated imagery

## VISUAL AESTHETIC STANDARDS

### Photography Style

#### Compositional Principles
- **Framing:** Often featuring architectural framing (gates, doorways, urban geometry)
- **Depth:** Multiple layers of silhouettes and environmental context
- **Perspective:** Street-level perspective, intimate camera angles
- **Symmetry:** Often centered composition with environmental symmetry

#### Lighting Approach
- **Key Light:** Directional, often from high angle or side
- **Fill Light:** Minimal, maintaining dramatic shadows
- **Rim Light:** Strong rim/edge lighting for character definition
- **Practical Lights:** Streetlights, neon, moonlight as visible sources
- **Ratio:** High contrast (3:1 to 5:1 minimum)

#### Color Grading
- **Desaturation:** Reduced saturation except for intentional accents
- **Contrast:** Crushed blacks, lifted midtones slightly
- **Temperature:** Slight cool bias with warm accent sources
- **Vignetting:** Subtle edge darkening to focus attention

### Imagery Subjects & Themes

#### Primary Visual Elements
- **Urban architecture:** Building facades, streets, industrial spaces
- **Silhouettes:** Characters backlit against environmental elements
- **Gates/Entrances:** Symbolic of boundaries and transitions
- **Moonlight:** Ethereal, ominous, contemplative atmosphere
- **Group dynamics:** Character relationships shown through positioning
- **Street culture:** Motorcycles, street wear, urban authenticity

#### Avoided Elements
- Bright, saturated colors (except story-specific sequences)
- Obvious computer graphics or digital effects
- Cheerful or uplifting imagery
- Clean, corporate aesthetics
- Clichéd urban drama tropes (graffiti-heavy, overly gritty)

## CHARACTER VISUAL IDENTITY

### Character Design Principles

#### Visual Hierarchy by Role
1. **Lead Protagonist:** Center-framed, strongest individual poster presence
2. **Key Supporting Characters:** Ensemble framing, strategic positioning
3. **Secondary Characters:** Background positioning, integrated into scenes
4. **Tertiary Characters:** Supporting roles within group compositions

#### Character Costuming Standards
- **Style:** Contemporary urban wear, street-authentic
- **Color Palette:** Black, gray, dark earth tones; metallic accents
- **Fit:** Realistic, not fashion-model styled
- **Authenticity:** Pieces that suggest real lives and economic realities
- **Character Indicators:** Clothing subtly indicates character background/role

#### Character Photography
- **Individual Portraits:** Direct or 3/4 angle, environmental context subtle
- **Group Arrangements:** Intentional spacing showing relationships
- **Environmental Integration:** Characters within street/urban locations
- **Lighting:** Consistent with overall brand aesthetic
- **Expression:** Authentic, contemplative, street-real (not smiling/posed)

## POSTER & PROMOTIONAL STANDARDS

### Primary Poster Composition

#### Central Poster Elements
- **Focal Point:** Moon or character silhouette (celestial/architectural framing)
- **Character Placement:** Strategic silhouettes around central element
- **Typography:** Title below or integrated with imagery
- **Taglines:** Positioned for visual balance and narrative impact
- **Credits:** Bottom placement, professional but minimal

#### Technical Specifications
- **Dimensions:** Standard 27" x 40" (theatrical poster size)
- **Resolution:** 300 DPI minimum for print
- **Format:** CMYK for print, RGB for digital
- **Color Space:** Consistent with brand palette

### Ensemble Poster Design

#### Layout Principles
- **Grid System:** Organized arrangement of cast members
- **Hierarchy:** Protagonist positioned prominently (center, larger, or elevated)
- **Spacing:** Intentional white space between figures
- **Arrangement:** Suggesting group dynamics or power structures

#### Information Hierarchy
- **Primary:** Series title and tagline
- **Secondary:** Cast names and character roles
- **Tertiary:** Production/network credits and air date

### Character Portrait Standards

#### Individual Character Poster
- **Single subject:** One character filling most of frame
- **Environment:** Urban location or suggestive background
- **Expression:** Character-authentic, aligned with established persona
- **Lighting:** Dramatic, consistent with brand aesthetic
- **Text:** Character name, role, one-line description

#### Multi-Character Groupings
- **Relationship Positioning:** Physical proximity suggesting connections
- **Narrative Implication:** Composition suggesting character dynamics
- **Visual Balance:** Professional arrangement without artificiality
- **Integration:** Characters naturally situated in environments

## DIGITAL & SOCIAL MEDIA STANDARDS

### Digital Asset Specifications

#### Image Formats
- **Primary:** High-resolution JPG (RGB, 72-300 DPI as needed)
- **Secondary:** PNG with transparency for overlays
- **Social Media:** Multiple aspect ratios:
  - **Facebook/Instagram Feed:** 1.91:1 (landscape), 4:5 (vertical)
  - **Stories:** 9:16 (vertical, full-screen)
  - **Twitter:** 16:9 (landscape)

#### Digital Color Space
- **Web/Screen:** RGB, sRGB color profile
- **Consistency:** Ensure colors match brand palette across devices
- **Contrast:** Minimum AA accessibility standards (4.5:1 text contrast)

### Social Media Posting Guidelines

#### Visual Consistency
- **Color Palette:** Always maintain brand metallic silver and deep black
- **Typography:** Use brand fonts where possible; fallback to sans-serif
- **Imagery:** Character photos and promotional posters only
- **Frequency:** Consistent posting schedule with brand-aligned content

#### Content Categories
1. **Character Spotlights:** Individual character posts with background
2. **Behind-the-Scenes:** Production photos (when available)
3. **Promotional Campaigns:** Poster reveals, tagline campaigns
4. **Engagement Posts:** Polls, questions using character imagery
5. **Countdown Posts:** Episode releases, major announcements

#### Hashtag Strategy
- **Primary Brand:** #DarkSquares #DarkSquaresTV
- **Character Tags:** #Mannie #Keith #Butch #Sarah #BoyGeorge
- **Engagement:** #TheStreets #TheEmpire #TheOath (from taglines)
- **Platform-Specific:** Adapt to platform conventions

## PRINT MATERIALS STANDARDS

### Poster & Print Specifications

#### Business Cards / Promotional Cards
- **Dimensions:** 3.5" x 2"
- **Stock:** Premium cardstock (100 lb cover minimum)
- **Colors:** Full color on front, reverse may be single-color
- **Text:** Logo, contact info, minimal branding

#### Outdoor Advertising
- **Billboard:** 14' x 48' standard
- **Digital Display:** Adapt image to screen specifications
- **Design:** Bold, readable from distance; simplified composition
- **Primary Focus:** Title and single visual element only

#### Merchandise (Shirts, Hats, etc.)
- **Logo Placement:** Chest, back, or hat front
- **Sizing:** Scale appropriately to garment
- **Colors:** Limited to 2-3 color printing where possible
- **Quality:** Professional embroidery or high-quality print

### Print Production Guidelines

#### Color Accuracy
- **Pantone Matching:** Use specific Pantone numbers for consistency
- **CMYK Conversion:** Verify conversions from RGB before print
- **Proofing:** Always request color proof before full production

#### Print Safety
- **Bleed:** Minimum 0.125" (1/8") bleed for all trim edges
- **Margins:** 0.25" minimum for text and important elements
- **Font:** Ensure all fonts are embedded or converted to outlines

## VIDEO & MOTION GRAPHICS STANDARDS

### Opening Sequence Guidelines

#### Visual Elements
- **Logo Reveal:** Metallic silver logo emerging from darkness
- **Typography Animation:** Tagline text appearing with intentional timing
- **Background:** Atmospheric urban imagery or abstract geometric elements
- **Duration:** 5-10 seconds for title sequence

#### Motion Principles
- **Pace:** Deliberate, not rushed
- **Ease:** Smooth, organic motion; avoid linear transitions
- **Sound:** Dramatic orchestral or atmospheric audio
- **Finale:** Logo settles with subtle audio cue

### Color Grade for Video

#### Standard Color Grade
- **LUT (Look-Up Table):** Consistent across all episodes
- **Black Level:** Crushed blacks for dramatic impact
- **Highlights:** Protected to retain detail in lit areas
- **Saturation:** Desaturated overall with subtle accent colors
- **Temperature:** Cool tones with practical light warmth

#### Scene-Specific Variations
- **Night Sequences:** Enhanced blacks, selective highlights
- **Day Sequences:** Desaturated naturalism
- **Flashbacks/Memories:** Slight color shift (cooler or warmer depending on tone)
- **Supernatural/Vision Sequences:** Stylized grade with intentional artificiality

## BRAND VOICE IN VISUAL DESIGN

### Visual Tone Checklist

- ✅ **Authentic:** Real-world urban authenticity
- ✅ **Sophisticated:** Professional, elevated aesthetic
- ✅ **Dramatic:** High contrast, intentional lighting
- ✅ **Consequential:** Imagery suggesting weight and stakes
- ✅ **Atmospheric:** Mood-driven over narrative-literal
- ✅ **Contemporary:** Current fashion, technology, cultural references
- ✅ **Diverse:** Authentic representation across cast and crew
- ✅ **Deliberate:** Every element serves the narrative

### Visual Tone Checklist - AVOID

- ❌ Overly stylized or unrealistic imagery
- ❌ Bright, cheerful color palettes
- ❌ Clichéd urban drama stereotypes
- ❌ Heavy-handed symbolic imagery
- ❌ Corporate or sanitized aesthetics
- ❌ Gratuitous darkness without narrative purpose
- ❌ Inconsistent visual language across materials
- ❌ Anything that contradicts brand positioning

## QUALITY ASSURANCE & APPROVAL WORKFLOW

### Design Review Checklist

Before any asset goes to production or distribution:

- ✅ Brand palette used correctly (no unauthorized color substitutions)
- ✅ Typography follows guidelines (correct fonts, sizing, spacing)
- ✅ Logo meets minimum size and clear space requirements
- ✅ Image resolution meets platform/print specifications
- ✅ Color space correct for intended use (CMYK for print, RGB for digital)
- ✅ Text contrast meets accessibility standards
- ✅ Composition aligns with established visual principles
- ✅ No brand elements distorted or misaligned
- ✅ Credits and legal information accurate and complete
- ✅ Cross-platform consistency verified

### Approval Process

1. **Design Phase:** Asset created per brand guidelines
2. **Internal Review:** Creative team verifies brand compliance
3. **Stakeholder Review:** Producer/network approval
4. **Final QA:** Confirm all corrections implemented
5. **Archive:** Save approved version with date and version number
6. **Distribution:** Release to appropriate channels
