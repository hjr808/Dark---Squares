## THEMATIC ELEMENTS

### Central Metaphor: Dark Squares
Throughout Book One, reinforce the concept that:
- Every choice creates a shadow
- These shadows have weight and consequence
- Some shadows become "dark squares" — permanent markers of moral choices
- The protagonist discovers this principle through lived experience

### Recurring Themes
- Loyalty vs. Ambition
- Survival instinct vs. Moral code
- Urban reality vs. Personal dreams
- Generational cycle and inherited burden

### Tone & Style
- Urban realism grounded in authentic detail
- Moral complexity without clear heroes or villains
- Consequence-focused narrative
- Character-driven internal conflict
- Contemporary street vernacular (authentic, not stereotypical)

## WRITING GUIDELINES FOR THIS MANUSCRIPT

### POV & Narrative Voice
- Narrative perspective: [First person / Close third person]
- Focus on protagonist's internal experience
- Occasional supporting character scenes (limited)
- Street-level perspective and observations

### Scene Construction
**Each scene should include:**
- Clear scene location and time
- Character(s) present
- Scene objective (what changes)
- Dialogue that reveals character
- Internal monologue/reaction
- Transition to next scene

### Dialogue Standards
- Authentic to character voice
- Avoid exposition dumps
- Subtext crucial to meaning
- Regional/cultural authenticity
- Realistic rhythm and interruptions

### Description & Atmosphere
- Urban setting details that establish place
- Sensory details (sight, sound, smell) that ground reader
- Lighting and mood consistent with Brand Guidelines
- Architecture and geography that matter to plot
- Avoid purple prose; lean toward noir aesthetic
