## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

**Dark Squares** is a multi-format entertainment project spanning television, novels, and supplementary materials. The project follows a young man navigating loyalty, ambition, and survival while discovering that every choice casts a shadow—and some shadows become dark squares that change lives forever.

**Project Scope:**
- TV Series (Multi-Season)
- Novel Series (3+ Books)
- Character Bibles & Supporting Materials
- Digital & Promotional Assets

---

## CREATIVE FOUNDATION

### Logline
A young man growing up between loyalty, ambition, and survival discovers that every choice casts a shadow—and some shadows become dark squares that change lives forever.

### Core Themes
- Moral complexity and consequence
- Loyalty vs. ambition
- Survival and adaptation
- The weight of decisions
- Urban struggle and resilience

## FILE MANAGEMENT GUIDELINES

### Naming Conventions
- Use underscores for multi-word filenames: `Book_One.md`, `Character_Development.md`
- Use descriptive names: avoid generic "draft1.md"
- Include version numbers when tracking iterations: `Book_One_v2.md`

### File Organization
- Keep related materials in dedicated directories
- Use consistent folder structure across seasons
- Maintain one master index (this file)

### Markdown Standards
- Use H1 for main titles, H2 for sections, H3 for subsections
- Include status badges (✅, 🔄, ⏳)
- Link to related files for easy navigation
- Include metadata (author, date, version) in headers

## NOTES & CONSIDERATIONS

### Thematic Through-Line
The concept of "dark squares" should permeate:
- Character decisions and consequences
- Plot structure and turning points
- Dialogue and internal monologue
- Visual/narrative metaphors
- Season-to-season progression

### Multi-Format Approach
- **Novel:** Extended narrative, internal monologue, detailed world-building
- **Television:** Visual storytelling, dialogue-driven, episodic structure
- **Supplementary:** Character bibles, timelines, behind-the-scenes context

### Tone & Style
- Urban realism
- Moral complexity without clear "good" or "bad"
- Consequences-focused narrative
- Character-driven conflict
