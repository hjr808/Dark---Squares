# DARK SQUARES

## Character Lookbook

### Created by Alfred HJR

**Scope:** Text-based casting and production guide for confirmed Book One / Season One characters. This document preserves the controlling screenplay names **Manny** and **Keith**; planning-only “Mannie” and unsupported “Li Keith” wording are not treated as canon.

---

## Series and Casting Philosophy

**Genre / tone:** Grounded urban coming-of-age, family, music, and ensemble friendship drama. Casting should privilege lived-in listening, humor, restraint, musical rhythm, and moral complexity over typecasting or exaggerated “street” performance.

**Visual and performance philosophy:** The Woods and the Rocks shape social behavior but do not predetermine worth. Wardrobe, hair, grooming, and behavior should be practical, period-appropriate to the unspecified contemporary setting, and rooted in scene function. Do not invent a dated era, named city, scars, tattoos, diagnoses, religion, sexuality, criminal history, or physical detail absent from the controlling record.

**Age handling:** Manny turns sixteen at the finale; Keith is four years younger. No exact birth years or exact ages for other characters are established. Cast ranges require Alfred HJR/production approval rather than invented numerical ages.

## MANNY

### Story Role
Protagonist, Ella Mae’s older son, Keith’s protective brother, basketball player, and developing DJ/producer.

### Confirmed Age or Age Range / Physical Description
Sixteen at the Season One finale; four years older than Keith. No height, skin tone, eye color, or other detailed physical description is confirmed in controlling production sources.

### Screen Presence, Personality, Voice, and Performance Notes
Observant, guarded, disciplined, protective, and introspective. He reads a situation before speaking and avoids needless escalation. Play the burden of responsibility through listening, pauses, court intelligence, and care for Keith—not speeches.

### Wardrobe / Hair / Grooming
Athletic, practical, and music-work ready; do not lock a signature look or unsupported grooming detail.

### Key Relationships / Season One Arc
Brother to Keith; son of Ella Mae; chosen brother and small-money partner to Butch; creative collaborator with Lucky; respectful, non-romantic connection with Sarah; Nate’s robbery-witness connection. Childhood protector → music/basketball growth → trusted Sweet Sixteen host unable to prevent the final consequence.

### Future Potential / Casting / Continuity Alerts
No New York path is established. Cast for basketball movement, quiet leadership, music listening, and brother chemistry. Keep the Manny spelling; do not create a Manny–Sarah romance or assign a height/light-skin detail.

## KEITH

### Story Role
Manny’s younger brother, social magnet, and natural freestyler.

### Confirmed Age or Age Range / Physical Description
Four years younger than Manny; exact age at the finale is not stated. No controlling physical description confirms skin tone or other measurements.

### Screen Presence, Personality, Voice, and Performance Notes
Funny, charismatic, crowd-facing, spontaneous, and rhythmically gifted. His freestyle is unprepared breath and connection, not polished performance. Play the contrast between social ease and the consequences of being pulled by the crowd.

### Wardrobe / Hair / Grooming
Youthful, mobile, expressive, and practical. Do not assign a dark-skin detail, hairstyle, or performance costume without approved source.

### Key Relationships / Season One Arc
Manny’s brother; Lucky’s studio confirms his ability; Chucky’s friend. Steering-wheel rhyme → practice tape → studio freestyle → Sarah’s recognition → Sweet Sixteen performance → remorse after the fight.

### Future Potential / Casting / Continuity Alerts
No post-finale music, legal, school, or emotional path is established. Cast for music timing and sibling chemistry. “Li Keith” is not an established name.

## BUTCH

### Story Role
Manny’s basketball rival turned chosen brother, musician, small-money partner, and Sarah’s established romantic connection.

### Confirmed Age or Age Range / Physical Description
From the Woods; dark-skinned, calm, sharp-eyed. Exact age and “Manny’s age” are not confirmed.

### Screen Presence, Personality, Voice, and Performance Notes
Patient, unshowy, sharp, network-aware, and musically broad. His voice is measured; he hears sadness in a chord and says, “I sing. I don’t rap.” Cast for basketball credibility, guitar/singing identity, and chemistry with Manny and Sarah.

### Wardrobe / Hair / Grooming
Practical, calm, individual—not a visual shorthand for money, criminal rise, or later status.

### Key Relationships / Season One Arc
Manny friendship begins with driveway basketball; Sarah meets him first at school. Friendship → music/money partnership → party protector who takes an ill guest home and tries to stop the fight.

### Future Potential / Continuity Alerts
No case, arrest, prison, release, or later rise exists. Preserve Sarah–Butch and no triangle.

## SARAH

### Story Role
High-school friend who joins the circle and becomes Butch’s romantic connection.

### Confirmed Age or Age Range / Physical Description
Light brown hair, green eyes, calm posture, soft but firm manner. No confirmed height, parentage, or exact age.

### Screen Presence, Personality, Voice, and Performance Notes
Listening, perceptive, steady, and nonjudgmental. She sees value in Lucky’s imperfect studio and tries to include Chucky. Play warmth without converting it into a Manny romance.

### Wardrobe / Hair / Grooming
Practical, self-possessed, nonsexualized; preserve the light-brown-hair/green-eye description where casting and styling support it.

### Key Relationships / Season One Arc
Meets Butch first; values the studio; receives a walk home from Butch; brings Manny a cake; helps clean; introduces Michelle to Chucky; witnesses finale aftermath.

### Future Potential / Continuity Alerts
No attack or recovery source exists. Cast for quiet intelligence and Butch chemistry.

## ELLA MAE

### Story Role / Presence
Working mother, guardian of the home boundary, and source of the family’s move to the Rocks. Practical, protective, and trusting where trust is earned.

### Confirmed Details and Direction
No surname, physical description, or fixed work profession is confirmed. Use lived working-parent practicality; cast for warmth, authority, and the ability to make “no drama or streets in my house” feel like care.

### Relationships / Arc / Alerts
Mother to Manny and Keith; trusts Candyman at the party. Apartment rule → Rocks house → party trust. Her response to the finale is not shown.

## Ensemble Chemistry Map

- **Manny / Butch:** Basketball respect becomes chosen brotherhood and partnership.
- **Manny / Keith:** Protective older brother versus younger brother’s crowd pull; sibling chemistry is central.
- **Butch / Sarah:** Sarah meets Butch first; their closeness is natural and non-betraying.
- **Ella Mae / sons:** Rule, work, trust, and family protection.
- **Candyman / family:** Trusted adult with humor and party authority.
- **Lucky / young artists:** Studio craft, recognition, and musical possibility.
- **Cheerio / Keith:** Party-mic handoff precedes Keith’s freestyle.
- **Nate / Butch:** Shared group membership only; no special dyad is established.
- **Chucky / Keith:** Invitation, exclusion, confrontation, and unresolved consequence.
- **Pretty Ricky / wider world:** Not established; approval required.
