## The Story

Manny and Keith grow up under Ella Mae’s rule: keep the streets outside the home. In the Woods, they learn danger and protection. In the Rocks, they gain a larger house and new tests. Basketball earns respect; music offers a language and possible exit. Manny learns turntables, Keith discovers freestyle, and Lucky’s studio gives their talent a place to grow.

Butch enters through a driveway basketball game and becomes Manny’s chosen brother, partner, and Sarah’s established romantic connection. Sarah sees value in their imperfect studio; Nate’s arrival, money pressure, street influence, and belonging keep changing what friendship costs.

Season One moves toward Manny’s Sweet Sixteen, where music, family, friendship, and exclusion collide. The question is what survival, loyalty, ambition, and success will ask them to sacrifice.

> **Naming note:** The controlling completed production record uses **Manny** and **Keith**. “Mannie” appears only in planning references; “Li Keith” is not an established character name and is not used as canon here.

## Why *Dark Squares*

Music is plot action, basketball is social language, and a family home is both refuge and pressure point. The narrator turns childhood memory into emotional context; the one established nursery-rhyme opening and Episode 21 Check Check messages sharpen the finale. “Dark squares” are moral marks left by choices—the distance between childhood innocence and adult consequence.

**Confirmed production focus:** family responsibility, friendship, music, basketball, survival, and ambition. **Future-development only:** a broader multi-generational canvas and recurring faith material require Alfred HJR-approved source authority; they are not established canon.

## Season One Hook

From the Woods to the Rocks, the circle forms around music, basketball, family, and belonging. Manny’s Sweet Sixteen begins as a celebration, then becomes **Bloody Sweet 16** when a rain-soaked confrontation leaves Chucky unresponsive. The season ends with a scratched record, a 911 call, and no invented aftermath.
