# CREATOR LETTER

To whoever is reading this —

*Dark Squares* started with something I already knew.

Growing up, you learn things nobody teaches you. You learn that loyalty has a price.
You learn that ambition can look exactly like betrayal from the wrong angle. You learn
that the choices you make before you are old enough to fully understand consequence
are still yours — and that some of them follow you the rest of your life.

I watched that happen. I watched it happen to people I grew up around, to friends,
to brothers. You watch a celebration become something else in a single night, and
there is no erasing it. That night becomes the marker. Before that night and after
that night. That is what a dark square is.

Manny and Keith are not biographies. They are composites of something real — the love
between brothers, the pull of the streets, the small studio in the back room where
music felt like freedom, the birthday party that changed everything. These are
characters built from truth.

What I wanted to write — and what I believe this series can do — is show that moral
complexity is not just a dramatic device. It lives in real people, in real families,
in real neighborhoods. The tragedy of *Bloody Sweet 16* is not that something bad
happened. The tragedy is that it was inevitable, that everyone could feel it coming
and still could not stop it.

That is the show.

This is a story about what it costs to grow up. About the price of a choice made in
a moment of anger, the permanence of one wrong second, and the people who have to
carry it afterward — the brother, the mother, the friends who were there.

It is also a story about music, basketball, loyalty, and the specific light that comes
off a streetlamp on a wet night in the neighborhood where you were raised.

I believe in this project. I have been building it carefully, with discipline, because
the people it represents deserve that care. The series has twenty-one episodes of
source material, a completed narrative arc for Season One, a mapped direction for
Season Two, and a world that can sustain the kind of long-form serialized drama that
changes the conversation about urban storytelling.

I am not looking for someone to translate this. I am looking for a collaborator who
understands what it already is.

If that is you, I am ready to talk.

**Alfred HJR**
Creator, *Dark Squares*

*"Every generation receives an omen."*
