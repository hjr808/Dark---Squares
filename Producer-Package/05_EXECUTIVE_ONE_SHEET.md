### The Series

Manny is protective, disciplined, and quietly ambitious. His younger brother Keith
is charismatic, funny, and easy to love — which makes him easy to follow into trouble.

Together they grow up in the Woods, navigating a neighborhood that teaches boys to
read faces before they can read a room. Music finds them early: Manny through a
friend's turntables, Keith through an instinct for freestyle that stops the room.
When Ella Mae moves the family to the Rocks, they earn their place in a new world
and slowly build a circle — Butch, Lucky, Nate, Sarah — bound by music, basketball,
and the specific trust of people who have seen each other in bad moments.

Season One ends at Manny's Sweet Sixteen.

What begins as a celebration — a basement full of food and noise, a backyard alive with
music — ends in the rain in front of Ella Mae's house, with Keith's friend Chucky
unresponsive on the concrete and Uncle Candyman calling 911.

The neighborhood will call it **Bloody Sweet 16** forever.

---

### Why Now

Urban television has evolved. Audiences are ready for a series that treats the moral
weight of street life with the same complexity and formal discipline that prestige
drama brings to any other world. *Dark Squares* does not exploit its setting — it
inhabits it. The music is real culture. The street language is exact. The love between
brothers is the architecture of every scene.

Season Two maps three years of aftermath. The franchise goes wherever the consequences
lead — which is exactly what makes it compelling.
