# SUPPORTING CHARACTER PROFILES

## Dark Squares — Season One

---

### SARAH
**Role:** High-school friend | Joins the circle | Butch's romantic partner

Sarah has light brown hair, green eyes, a calm posture, and a soft but firm manner.
She meets Butch first at the high-school lockers. She comes to know Manny, Keith,
Lucky, and Nate gradually. She does not judge the Woods or perform amazement at the
Rocks. She listens and recognizes that the studio is meaningful because it belongs
to its creators.

She does not fit the circle by accident. She earns her place by being exactly who
she is — observant, honest, and steady under pressure.

She makes Manny a homemade chocolate cake for his sixteenth birthday (Episode 18,
*The Chocolate Cake*). It is a private gesture. She and Manny share a quiet personal
connection that remains unconsummated throughout Season One.

At the party, she helps Manny clean during the after-party, checks on Chucky,
introduces him to Michelle, and witnesses the fatal-seeming outcome of the fight.
She is one of the people who was there.

---

### NATE / NUTTY NATE
**Role:** Witness who becomes part of the group

Manny first meets Nate after two older boys rob Manny of his Max Julian coat at a
bus stop in winter. Nate is witness to that encounter. Later, at Coney Island
(Episode 13), Nate joins the group and walks with them to Lucky's studio. He is no
longer just a witness — he is a member.

By the party in Episode 21, he is Nutty Nate to the group. He brings alcohol to the
party, warns that it is strong, and messages on Check Check as Keith and Chucky's
tension rises. His Check Check thread is evidence of what the night felt like from
inside it — and evidence of the tension before the fight.

---

### KIZZY
**Role:** The brothers' dog | Companion through the Woods years

Kizzy is part of the crew. She accompanies Manny and Keith on their adventures,
barks at shadows, chases bikes, alerts the boys to danger, and is treated with
the full loyalty they give each other. She is present through the Woods episodes
and is part of how those years feel — alive, vigilant, and briefly free.

---

### CHUCKY
**Role:** Keith's friend | The fight | The climax

Chucky is part of Keith's earlier social world in the Woods. He attends Manny's
Sweet Sixteen because Keith invites him. He feels welcomed briefly — Sarah introduces
him to Michelle, and they dance. He promises to take Michelle shopping.

But the party pulls Keith elsewhere. The invitation was real; the attention was not
sustained. Chucky sits at the edges. After the last dance, the feeling of being
left out becomes unbearable.

He confronts Keith about it. The argument moves outside into the rain. The fight
happens. Keith lifts him and slams him onto the concrete.

Season One ends with Chucky unresponsive. His medical outcome, legal situation, and
subsequent story belong to Season Two and are not established in Season One canon.

---

### UNCLE CANDYMAN
**Role:** Ella Mae's trusted adult | Party chaperone | Street philosopher

Uncle Candyman is designated by Ella Mae to keep order at the Sweet Sixteen while
she works. He is a neighborhood figure — trusted adult, street philosopher, keeper
of the informal rules that govern how a party like this is supposed to run.

He is the one who calls 911 after Keith slams Chucky onto the concrete. He is present
in the late-party sequence as one of the adults who watched everything happen.

---

### STEVE
**Role:** Turntable owner | Manny's musical doorway | Introduction to Lucky

Steve owns the turntables that first teach Manny to blend records. He is Manny's
entry point to music as craft rather than entertainment. He later introduces Manny
to Lucky, which sets the group's creative center in motion. Steve's role is
foundational: without Steve, there is no Lucky's room, no Keith's freestyle discovery.

---

### CHEERIO
**Role:** Microphone before Keith at the Sweet Sixteen

Cheerio takes the microphone at the party before Keith. He is part of the studio
circle, present at Lucky's room. His mic moment sets the stage for Keith's freestyle.

---

### MICHELLE
**Role:** Chucky's brief connection at the party

Michelle is introduced to Chucky by Sarah at the Sweet Sixteen. She dances with him.
For a time, Chucky has a reason to stay and a sense of belonging. He promises to
take her shopping. After the last dance, that connection is not enough to hold
what comes next.

---

### OLD MAN WILLIE
**Role:** Neighborhood character | The dope fein dinner

Old Man Willie trades a grill for something he needs. The transaction — and the
specific humor of it — appears in Episode 11 (*Dope Fein Dinner*). He is part of
the neighborhood's street economy.

---

### BOY GEORGE
**Note:** Boy George appears in character planning and relationship maps as a street
connection in Manny's world. He does not have a detailed scene-by-scene profile in
the Season One narrative manuscript. His role and relationship to the core group
should not be expanded without approved source material.
