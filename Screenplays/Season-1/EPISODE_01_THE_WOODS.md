# DARK SQUARES
## Episode 1 — "THE WOODS"

**Television Screenplay Adaptation**

---

## TEASER

**FADE IN:**

### EXT. THE WOODS — NIGHT

Streetlights blink on over a four-family building, alleys, porches, and an empty lot. Music leaks from car windows and porch radios. Two boys on bikes cut through the block: **MANNY**, watchful even while riding, and **KEITH**, laughing too loud. Their little dog, **KIZZY**, races behind them.

**MANNY (V.O.)**
Before the Rocks, before Lucky's studio, before Sarah, before the party that would carry a name nobody wanted to say out loud, there was the Woods.

### EXT. THE WOODS — CONTINUOUS

The brothers fly past a stickball game, a basketball bouncing on cracked pavement, older boys carrying a boom box.

**MANNY (V.O.)**
The Woods was not just a neighborhood. It was a classroom, a warning, a playground, and a test.

Keith swerves around Manny, grinning. Manny reaches out, steadying him before he can hit a curb.

**MANNY (V.O.)**
It taught boys how to run, how to hide, how to laugh when nothing was funny, and how to read a face before a word came out of a mouth.

Kizzy barks at a shadow. The boys laugh and keep moving.

**CUT TO TITLE: DARK SQUARES**

---

## ACT ONE

### INT. ELLA MAE'S APARTMENT — EVENING

Thin walls. A low apartment near the basement hall, the laundry room, and the back entrance. Every footstep in the building travels through it.

**ELLA MAE** stands before Manny and Keith, work bag at her shoulder. She looks at them until they are listening.

**ELLA MAE**
Do not bring drama or the streets to this house.

Manny nods. Keith follows his brother's lead.

**MANNY (V.O.)**
She did not say it like a suggestion. She said it like scripture.

Ella Mae looks around her living room: the one place where the outside world is supposed to stop at the door.

**MANNY (V.O.)**
Whatever happened outside stayed outside. Whatever pride, disrespect, misunderstanding, or trouble followed a person home had to die before it reached her living room.

Ella Mae exits for work. The boys wait until the outside door closes.

Keith looks to Manny.

**KEITH**
We outside?

Manny gives him a look that says *you already know.* Keith smiles.

### EXT. THE WOODS — LATER

Manny and Keith ride bikes. They join a stickball game, then a basketball game, then disappear down an alley. Kizzy stays with them.

**MANNY (V.O.)**
Manny understood that rule better than anyone. He was the oldest. Keith was his little brother. Four years separated them, but in the Woods, four years could feel like a whole lifetime.

Manny watches Keith climb a fence toward an empty lot. He follows, not because he wants to, but because Keith is already going.

**MANNY (V.O.)**
Manny was not Keith's father, but he carried pieces of that job anyway.

---

## ACT TWO

### EXT. EMPTY LOT / ALLEY — NIGHT

The streetlights are on. The boys should be home. Instead, they explore the edge of the lot while Kizzy noses through weeds.

**MANNY (V.O.)**
When Ella Mae worked late, the boys had more freedom than they should have had. The neighborhood opened up after the streetlights came on.

Keith hears the boom box from the corner and starts moving to the beat. He tries to make Manny laugh with a ridiculous dance. Manny does, despite himself.

**MANNY (V.O.)**
Music was already there, even then. Coming from car windows. From radios on porches. From older boys carrying boom boxes.

Manny and Keith pause at the mouth of the alley, listening.

**MANNY (V.O.)**
Hip-hop was not background noise to them. It was weather. It was the sound of growing up.

Older kids call Keith over. Keith heads toward them without thinking. Manny catches the back of his shirt.

**MANNY**
Not over there.

**KEITH**
I'm just talking.

**MANNY**
Talk from here.

Keith rolls his eyes, but stays. Kizzy plants herself beside Manny's leg.

**MANNY (V.O.)**
Keith followed crowds easily. People liked him. He could make anybody laugh, and that gift made him welcome in places Manny did not always want him to be.

Keith makes a face at Manny, then makes Kizzy chase him back toward home.

**MANNY (V.O.)**
Some boys attract friends. Some attract trouble. Keith had a way of attracting both.

---

## TAG

### EXT. THE WOODS — NIGHT

The brothers ride side by side beneath the streetlights. Their building waits ahead. Kizzy runs between their bikes.

**MANNY (V.O.)**
In those days, the Woods felt bigger than the whole world.

Keith laughs at something only he understands. Manny watches him, protective and quiet.

**MANNY (V.O.)**
They had no idea they would one day move away from it.

The boys disappear into the dark entrance of their building.

**MANNY (V.O.)**
They had no idea the Woods would follow them anyway.

**FADE OUT.**

**END OF EPISODE**
