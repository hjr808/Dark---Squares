# DARK SQUARES
## Episode 3 — "JUNKYARD KINGS"

**Television Screenplay Adaptation**

---

## TEASER

### EXT. JUNKYARD — DAY

Rows of used cars, broken glass, rust, and warning signs. Manny and Keith slip through an opening in the fence. Kizzy follows.

**MANNY (V.O.)**
There was a junkyard near the alley that felt like a secret kingdom.

Keith climbs into a wrecked car. Manny takes in the place like it belongs to them.

**MANNY (V.O.)**
To grown folks, it was a place full of used cars, broken glass, rust, and warning signs. To Manny and Keith, it was imagination with wheels.

**CUT TO TITLE: DARK SQUARES**

---

## ACT ONE

### INT. WRECKED CAR — DAY

Keith slides across a torn leather seat.

**KEITH**
This one mine.

Manny looks at the missing door.

**MANNY**
That one ain't even got a door.

**KEITH**
Don't need one. Race car.

Manny climbs into a faded sedan with a cracked dashboard.

**MANNY**
This mine. Smooth.

**KEITH**
That look like something a teacher drive.

**MANNY**
A rich teacher.

They laugh so loud birds scatter from the fence.

### EXT. JUNKYARD — LATER

The brothers move from car to car, pretending to drive across the country. Kizzy jumps around their feet.

**MANNY (V.O.)**
The junkyard was one of the few places where Keith belonged only to Manny. No Chucky. No older boys. No crowd pulling him somewhere else.

Manny and Keith look out over the rows of wrecks.

**MANNY (V.O.)**
Just two brothers making a world out of what other people threw away.

---

## ACT TWO

### INT. FADED SEDAN — DAY

Manny taps a rhythm on the steering wheel. Keith talks over it, turning nonsense into a rough little rap.

**MANNY**
You always making noise.

**KEITH**
You always making beats.

**MANNY**
That ain't a beat.

**KEITH**
It is when I rap on it.

Manny shakes his head, but he keeps tapping. Keith grins, continuing his nonsense rhyme.

**MANNY (V.O.)**
Keith would rap nonsense over the beat without knowing he was practicing for a future that had not found him yet.

The light changes. The distant streetlights flicker on.

Manny stops tapping.

**MANNY**
Come on.

### EXT. ALLEY / THE WOODS — DUSK

The brothers head home. Kizzy trots behind them, barking at shadows and chasing leaves.

**MANNY (V.O.)**
When the streetlights came on, you better already be close to home.

Kizzy barks at nothing. Keith stops.

**KEITH**
She don't see no ghosts?

**MANNY**
She don't see no ghosts.

**KEITH**
Then why she barking at nothing?

**MANNY**
Because she crazy like you.

Keith laughs and runs ahead. Kizzy takes off after him.

---

## TAG

### EXT. ALLEY — DUSK

Manny watches Keith and Kizzy race ahead through the fading light. He smiles.

**MANNY (V.O.)**
He did not know yet that these ordinary days would become sacred later.

The three figures move down the alley: a boy, his little brother, and their dog.

**MANNY (V.O.)**
He did not know that one day he would give anything to ride through that alley again with his little brother and that dog, pretending broken things could still take them somewhere.

**FADE OUT.**

**END OF EPISODE**
