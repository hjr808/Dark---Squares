# DARK SQUARES
## Episode 6 — "NIGHT LIGHTS"

**Television Screenplay Adaptation**

---

## TEASER

**FADE IN:**

### EXT. THE WOODS — DUSK

Streetlights wait over bikes against fences, a basketball in a driveway, and a football in somebody's hands.

**MANNY (V.O.)**
Everybody had a curfew. That was the rule in the neighborhood.

The lights SNAP ON.

**MANNY (V.O.)**
When the lights came on, you started heading home. Not later. Not after one more game. Not after one more lap around the block. Home.

Manny and Keith look toward the apartment. No Ella Mae.

**MANNY (V.O.)**
But rules changed on the nights Ella Mae worked late.

**CUT TO TITLE: DARK SQUARES**

---

## ACT ONE

### EXT. THE WOODS — EARLY EVENING

Keith moves through a group of older boys, laughing at jokes he is too young to tell and stories he is too young to understand. Manny watches from a distance, never too far away.

**MANNY (V.O.)**
Those nights felt like borrowed time. The neighborhood became its own world after dark.

Kizzy barks at a boy who gets too close, then chases a bike past the porch and back again.

**MANNY (V.O.)**
It was not always safe. But it was alive.

Keith catches Manny looking.

**KEITH**
What?

Manny only shakes his head.

**MANNY (V.O.)**
He was older, but not grown. Still, in Keith's life, he was the closest thing to a father figure the boy had every day.

Manny keeps an eye on Keith as the group shifts around him.

**MANNY (V.O.)**
That made Manny's childhood heavier. He could not just be outside. He had to monitor the outside.

---

## ACT TWO

### EXT. THE WOODS — LATE AFTERNOON TO DUSK

One Friday stretches itself out.

— KICKBALL becomes STICKBALL.

— STICKBALL becomes BASKETBALL.

— BASKETBALL becomes a BIKE RACE.

Kizzy barks at anyone who comes too close, guards the porch, then tears after the boys on their bikes.

**MANNY (V.O.)**
Kizzy made that job easier and harder at the same time. She barked at anyone who came too close, chased bikes, guarded the porch, and sometimes made more noise than the boys she was supposed to protect.

The sky turns purple. The streetlights come on.

Keith looks at Manny. Manny looks toward the house. Ella Mae is still at work.

Keith smiles.

**KEITH**
One more ride?

Manny should say no. Instead, he climbs onto his bike.

**MANNY**
One more.

---

## TAG

### EXT. THE WOODS — NIGHT

Manny and Keith ride through the neighborhood with Kizzy racing alongside. For a little while, the night belongs to them.

**MANNY (V.O.)**
They rode through the neighborhood like the night belonged to them. For a little while, it did.

The bikes disappear beneath the streetlights.

**MANNY (V.O.)**
That was the danger of freedom. It made boys believe they could outrun consequences.

**FADE OUT.**

**END OF EPISODE**
