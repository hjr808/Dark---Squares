# DARK SQUARES
## Episode 11 — "DOPE FEIN DINNER"

**Television Screenplay Adaptation**

---

## TEASER

**FADE IN:**

### EXT. TRAP HOUSE — NIGHT

Smoke rises from a grill that has no business being outside this house. Steaks and potatoes sizzle. **OLD MAN WILLIE** works the grill while Manny and Butch take in the scene.

**MANNY (V.O.)**
The trap house smelled like charcoal and bad decisions.

**MANNY (V.O.)**
Old Man Willie had traded a grill for something he needed more than a grill. That was the kind of exchange the neighborhood understood without anyone writing it down.

Somebody looks at the food and laughs.

**VOICE #1**
Steaks. Potatoes.

**VOICE #2**
Dope fiend dinner.

Laughter moves through the yard.

**CUT TO TITLE: DARK SQUARES**

---

## ACT ONE

### EXT. TRAP HOUSE — CONTINUOUS

Plates pass through the group. Smoke rises. The joke grows.

**VOICE #3**
First mobile delivery service.

**VOICE #1**
Before Uber.

**VOICE #2**
Before DoorDash.

The room laughs because the comparison is ridiculous and true in the only way neighborhood jokes have to be true.

Manny smiles, then watches Butch move through the scene. Butch is not reckless. He is familiar.

**MANNY (V.O.)**
Butch moved through that world differently than Manny did. More comfortable. More fluent. Not reckless, exactly. Just familiar.

The laughter continues, but Manny sees the warning beneath it.

**MANNY (V.O.)**
There was humor in the scene, but there was also warning.

---

## ACT TWO

### EXT. TRAP HOUSE — LATER

Manny and Butch stand a little apart from the grill. Cash changes hands elsewhere. A name gets remembered. A favor is acknowledged.

**MANNY (V.O.)**
The boys were getting older. Their jokes were changing. Their access was changing. Their understanding of money was changing.

Images pass between them: a few dollars, a favor, a package delivered, a name remembered.

**MANNY (V.O.)**
Nothing felt like a fall when it started. It felt like helping. It felt like growing up. It felt like finally having money in your pocket that did not come from asking your mother.

Butch gives Manny a small share. Manny takes it. No display. No empire. Just enough.

**MANNY (V.O.)**
Manny and Butch became partners in small ways first. Nothing flashy. Nothing that looked like empire. Just enough money to buy shoes, help out, move with confidence.

Manny closes his hand around the money.

**MANNY (V.O.)**
But money has a voice. Once boys hear it, they rarely forget the sound.

---

## TAG

### EXT. TRAP HOUSE — NIGHT

Manny and Butch eat by the grill. The food tastes better than it should. The laughter lasts longer than it needs to.

Manny looks past the smoke, aware of something changing without anyone naming it.

**MANNY (V.O.)**
And somewhere beneath the joke, the Square quietly marked another moment.

The grill crackles.

**MANNY (V.O.)**
Not because anyone died. Because something innocent did.

**FADE OUT.**

**END OF EPISODE**
