# DARK SQUARES
## Episode 15 — "SARAH"

**Television Screenplay Adaptation**

---

## TEASER

**FADE IN:**

### INT. LUCKY'S ROOM / STUDIO — DAY

Sarah steps into the studio and takes it in: cassettes stacked by the wall, handwritten lyrics, a complicated mixer, microphones that have carried more dreams than finished songs.

**MANNY (V.O.)**
Sarah looked around like she had entered a museum made by teenagers.

Lucky watches her reaction carefully.

**LUCKY**
What you think?

Sarah smiles.

**SARAH**
I think it's perfect.

Lucky laughs.

**LUCKY**
It ain't perfect.

**SARAH**
No. But it's yours.

Lucky holds her answer.

**CUT TO TITLE: DARK SQUARES**

---

## ACT ONE

### INT. LUCKY'S ROOM / STUDIO — CONTINUOUS

Butch watches Sarah watch the room. She does not make fun of the small walls or mismatched equipment.

**MANNY (V.O.)**
She understood that what mattered was not how polished the place looked. It was what happened inside it.

Keith keeps away from the microphone.

**KEITH**
I ain't doing it.

Lucky drops a beat. Somebody laughs. The rhythm pulls Keith in anyway.

Keith freestyles, unplanned and easy. Sarah listens without interrupting.

When he finishes, she looks at him.

**SARAH**
You really don't write?

Keith shrugs.

**KEITH**
Nah.

**SARAH**
That's incredible.

Keith looks embarrassed. Manny smiles, proud.

**MANNY**
I heard it before anybody.

---

## ACT TWO

### INT. LUCKY'S ROOM / STUDIO — LATER

Sarah looks around the room: Manny, basketball player and producer; Butch, music in his bones and money in his mind; Lucky building sound from scraps; Keith speaking thoughts into rhythm; Nate quiet and present.

**SARAH**
None of you know how talented you are,

Nobody answers because nobody believes her yet.

Butch and Sarah share a smile. Manny sees it and says nothing.

### EXT. SARAH'S STREET — EVENING

Butch walks Sarah home. He does not rush the moment.

Sarah notices.

**MANNY (V.O.)**
That was another thing she liked.

---

## TAG

### EXT. SARAH'S STREET — EVENING

Butch and Sarah continue down the block, talking easily. Manny's narration holds the relationship in its true shape.

**MANNY (V.O.)**
Over time, she and Butch became close in a way that felt natural. He made her laugh. She made him softer without asking him to change. Manny saw it and respected it.

Butch and Sarah walk on together.

**MANNY (V.O.)**
There was no betrayal in that. Just youth. Just timing. Just three lives beginning to tie themselves together before any of them understood how hard the knots would be to untangle.

**FADE OUT.**

**END OF EPISODE**
