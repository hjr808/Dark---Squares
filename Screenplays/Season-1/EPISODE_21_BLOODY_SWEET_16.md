# DARK SQUARES
## Episode 21 — "BLOODY SWEET 16"

**Television Screenplay Adaptation — Season One Finale**

---

## TEASER

**FADE IN:**

### EXT. ELLA MAE'S BACKYARD — WET NIGHT

**MANNY (V.O.)**
Old MacDonald had a farm... E-I-E-I-O...

Children's voices echo through the dark yard.

**MANNY (V.O.)**
Children sing songs before they understand what endings are. They clap. They laugh. They repeat the same words over and over, believing the world is simple because nobody has told them different yet.

The yard is nearly empty. The last record plays low.

**MANNY (V.O.)**
But childhood does not always end slowly. Sometimes it ends in the rain. Sometimes it ends on concrete.

The birthday banner hangs from the porch.

**MANNY (V.O.)**
That night had started as Manny's Sweet Sixteen. By morning, the neighborhood would call it something else.

**CUT TO TITLE: DARK SQUARES**

---

## ACT ONE

### EXT. ELLA MAE'S BACKYARD — CONTINUOUS

Nutty Nate stands at the edge of the yard, phone in hand. He watches Keith, then Chucky. The air shifts again.

Nate opens **CHECK CHECK** and types.

> **Nutty Nate:** They look like they about to get into it.

He looks up. Keith and Chucky face one another. Nate types again.

> **Nutty Nate:** What you think?

Before any answer matters, Keith speaks.

**KEITH**
Man... what your problem?

**CHUCKY**
I ain't got no problem.

**KEITH**
You sure?

**CHUCKY**
I'm positive.

**KEITH**
You been acting funny all night.

Chucky steps closer.

**CHUCKY**
Funny? Nah. I been watching.

**KEITH**
Watching what?

**CHUCKY**
You.

**KEITH**
Me?

**CHUCKY**
Yeah. You invite me here, then act like I don't exist.

**KEITH**
It wasn't like that.

**CHUCKY**
It was to me.

Lucky's record plays low: the last song, the last groove, the last piece of a perfect night.

---

## ACT TWO

### EXT. ELLA MAE'S BACKYARD — CONTINUOUS

Keith takes a breath.

**KEITH**
Chuck, I was helping my brother. It was his birthday.

**CHUCKY**
I know whose birthday it was.

**KEITH**
Then why you acting like this?

**CHUCKY**
Because everybody made me feel welcome except the person who invited me.

The words land. Keith looks down. For a second, the night could turn back.

**CHUCKY**
Man, forget it.

Chucky turns away.

**KEITH**
Chuck, hold up.

Chucky keeps moving. Rain begins—first drops, then more.

### EXT. FRONT OF ELLA MAE'S HOUSE — CONTINUOUS

Rain falls steadily. The concrete shines beneath the porch light.

**CHUCKY**
Leave me alone.

**KEITH**
I'm trying to talk to you.

**CHUCKY**
You should've talked to me all night.

Keith grabs his arm. Chucky jerks away.

**CHUCKY**
Don't touch me.

Keith steps in front of him.

**KEITH**
Man, stop running.

**CHUCKY**
I ain't running from nobody.

The shove comes fast. Then another. Then hands. Then swinging.

Manny runs from the side of the house.

**MANNY**
Keith!

Butch moves faster.

**BUTCH**
Break it up!

Uncle Candyman shouts from the driveway.

**UNCLE CANDYMAN**
Enough!

---

## ACT THREE

### EXT. FRONT OF ELLA MAE'S HOUSE — CONTINUOUS

Rain makes everything slick. Keith and Chucky lock up. Chucky tries to twist away. Keith catches him around the waist.

For one second, everything freezes.

**SARAH**
No!

Keith lifts. Turns. Slams Chucky onto the concrete.

The sound cracks through the rain. Not loud. Final.

The yard goes silent. Behind the house, the music keeps spinning. Lucky's needle scratches the same groove again and again.

Keith staggers back, hands trembling.

**KEITH**
Chuck...

Chucky does not answer.

Manny drops to his knees.

**MANNY**
Chucky!

Michelle screams from the sidewalk. Sarah covers her mouth and backs into Butch.

Uncle Candyman pushes through the group.

**UNCLE CANDYMAN**
Call 911!

Nobody moves at first. Not because they did not hear him. Because the world has stopped making sense.

---

## TAG

### EXT. FRONT OF ELLA MAE'S HOUSE — CONTINUOUS

Rain washes across the driveway, carrying thin red streaks toward the gutter. The birthday banner hangs crooked. One balloon breaks loose and floats into the street.

Keith stares at his hands.

**KEITH**
I didn't mean...

His voice breaks.

**KEITH**
I didn't mean to...

Manny looks up at his little brother. There is nothing he can say.

**MANNY (V.O.)**
No big brother lesson. No street rule. No music. No basketball. No birthday wish. Nothing could undo what had just happened.

The rain keeps falling. The record keeps scratching.

**MANNY (V.O.)**
The nursery rhyme was gone now. Childhood was gone with it.

**MANNY (V.O.)**
And Manny's Sweet Sixteen became the night everyone would remember as Bloody Sweet 16.

**FADE OUT.**
