## 1. Production Rules and Canon Boundary

- This bible indexes the completed Season One adaptations; it does **not** rewrite, expand, or supersede them.
- The city and calendar year are deliberately unspecified. Keep the urban setting specific in texture, not in named geography.
- Use **Manny**, the spelling used in the detailed manuscript and adaptations.
- Maintain street-level realism: humor, music, family warmth, threat, and consequence coexist. Do not turn the neighborhood into caricature.
- Music is action, not background: records, turntables, DJ choices, mixing, and freestyle alter the emotional state of scenes.
- The Season One endpoint is limited to Chucky being unresponsive after Keith slams him onto concrete. Death, medical outcome, investigation, arrest, charges, and legal aftermath are **not established** and must not be assumed.
- No substantive standalone Character Bible, World Bible, or Continuity Log file exists in the repository. The Master Canon supplies the available character/world continuity verification.

4. The Rocks house is structurally important: garage, basement, backyard, fence, porch, and front driveway each receive later story use.

## 5. Relationships Established and Family Progression

### Permanent / continuing relationships

- **Manny–Keith:** brothers; Manny is protective and carries father-like responsibility when Ella Mae works. Manny leans toward craft/discipline, Keith toward crowd/connection.
- **Manny–Butch:** driveway basketball respect becomes friendship, then small-money partnership. They are not blood relatives.
- **Manny–Lucky:** creative collaborators; Lucky advances Manny's production education.
- **Keith–Lucky:** Lucky's studio confirms Keith's natural freestyle ability.
- **Butch–Sarah:** Sarah meets Butch first; their closeness grows naturally. This is the established romance.
- **Sarah–Manny:** mutual respect and a personal connection around cake/cleaning, but no established romance, betrayal, or triangle.
- **Keith–Chucky:** Keith's broad invitation becomes a perceived promise; party inattention becomes Chucky's sense of rejection.
- **Manny–Nate:** robbery witness connection becomes quiet recognition, then group membership.

### Family storyline

| Phase | Progression |
|---|---|
| Woods childhood | Ella Mae works, Manny looks after Keith, and Kizzy reinforces the brothers' small family unit. Ella Mae's rule: no drama or streets in the house. |
| Freedom/constraint | Curfew matters; Ella Mae's late shifts create dangerous borrowed time. |
| Material progress | Ella Mae finds a house in the Rocks: space, basement, garage, yard, fence, and a front door belonging to the family. |
| Birthday trust | Ella Mae leaves Manny responsible for the Sweet Sixteen and Candyman as chaperone. Manny cleans because it is her house. |
| Finale impact | The final fight occurs at the front of Ella Mae's house, turning the protected family milestone into Bloody Sweet 16. Ella Mae's response is not established. |

## 11. Unresolved Storylines and Future-Season Adaptation Notes

### Unresolved at the end of Season One

- Chucky's condition, survival, and outcome.
- The immediate response of Ella Mae and the larger family/community.
- Any police, medical, legal, school, or neighborhood consequences.
- Whether Manny and Butch's small-money partnership escalates, ends, or costs them more.
- The long-term effect of Keith's talent, crowd pull, and final action.
- The long-term future of Manny's music, Lucky's studio, and Butch/Sarah's relationship.
- Nate's place in the group after the party.

### Future-season continuity rules

- Do not retroactively state that Chucky died; the authored endpoint is only unresponsiveness.
- Do not add an investigation, arrest, conviction, funeral, or hospital result as if it occurred in Season One.
- Keep the causal chain intact: the catastrophe comes from exclusion, misread invitation, delayed attention, rain, and physical escalation—not a pre-established feud or secret motive.
- Preserve Ella Mae's house as a moral boundary; the tragedy's location is central to its cost.
- Preserve the distinction between the Woods and the Rocks: progress changes physical space, not social history.
- Preserve Sarah–Butch as the romantic relationship; Manny–Sarah remains personal but unconsummated and non-triangular.
- Treat music and basketball as character language in future adaptation: Manny's craft, Keith's voice, Lucky's technical space, and Butch's musical depth all remain active continuity.
- Maintain narrator compression and reflective endings; avoid replacing these with exposition that resolves unestablished facts.
