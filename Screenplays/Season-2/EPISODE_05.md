# DARK SQUARES

## SEASON TWO, EPISODE 05: “COME HOME”

**One-hour serialized drama — Production Draft**  
**Timeline:** Three years after Bloody Sweet 16.  
**Logline:** Three years after Bloody Sweet 16, Li Keith’s first public performance opens a path forward while Sarah claims her own voice, Chucky demands to be heard, and Butch decides what his growing power will mean.

---

## TEASER

### EXT. ELLA MAE’S HOUSE, ROCKS — NIGHT
Li Keith performs “Come Home” at the youth showcase. A local DJ offers Lucky a supervised recording session; Chucky asks his mother to call Keith because he does not want his story told wrong.

**UNCLE CANDYMAN**  
Nobody runs. Nobody touches him. The truth is what it is.

**KEITH**  
I didn’t mean to. Mannie, I didn’t mean to.

### INT. ELLA MAE’S HOUSE — PRE-DAWN
Police separate the teenagers in the wreckage of the Sweet Sixteen. Lucky quietly removes Nate’s bottle and his own protection from the room; Mannie watches the ambulance take Chucky away. Ella Mae comes home to tape, rain, and a house that no longer feels private.

**ELLA MAE**  
I gave you one rule: the street stays outside this house.

**MANNIE**  
It found the porch.

---

## ACT ONE

### INT. HOSPITAL WAITING AREA — MORNING
Sarah tells police she wants evidence handled as evidence, not as neighborhood gossip. Keith visits Chucky and listens to him without asking forgiveness. Ella Mae lets herself be proud of Keith’s music while insisting that celebration cannot erase the past.

**DOCTOR**  
He is alive. He is critical. The next hours matter.

**SARAH**  
Alive is not the same as all right.

### EXT. HOSPITAL — MORNING
Butch gets Michelle safely home, then refuses a call from a man eager to turn the night into neighborhood business. Nate and Cheerio leave when Candyman tells them to. Mannie stays beside Keith.

**BUTCH**  
People will write their own version if we let them.

**MANNIE**  
We don’t get to write it.

### INT. ELLA MAE’S KITCHEN — DAY
Ella Mae makes calls to parents while Mannie scrubs blood from the front step. Keith cannot eat. Candyman reminds Mannie that being the older brother does not make him the father.

---

## ACT TWO

### INT. HIGH SCHOOL HALLWAY — DAY
Mannie and Butch end their street-money partnership. Butch converts his informal protection into an open weekly basketball court; Boy George is picked up on an unrelated warrant and predicts people will still come to Butch.

**SARAH**  
Truth doesn’t get softer because we whisper it.

### INT. LUCKY’S STUDIO — NIGHT
Lucky gives Keith a task before he gives him a microphone. The room becomes a workshop rather than a hiding place. Mannie insists that any song must carry responsibility, not turn pain into a boast.

**LUCKY**  
A beat can hold a feeling. It can’t excuse one.

### EXT. ROCKS CORNER — NIGHT
Boy George, an older neighborhood connector, tests Butch with talk of “steady hands” and paid order. Butch refuses the offer, but Mannie sees people already asking Butch to settle disputes.

---

## ACT THREE

### INT. ELLA MAE’S HOUSE — NIGHT
Sarah and Butch choose to walk beside each other slowly. Mannie accepts that his responsibility is to grow up, not replace Ella Mae. He answers the studio’s call: the brothers will go together.

**ELLA MAE**  
I need help. I don’t need to lose you to helping.

### EXT. GARAGE COURT — DAY
Butch calms a basketball argument without throwing a punch. Nate notices the neighborhood listens. Mannie warns that influence has a price; Butch agrees, but cannot pretend it is not already arriving.

### INT. HOSPITAL ROOM — DAY
Chucky’s mother permits a brief visit or message. Keith offers an apology without asking to be forgiven. Chucky’s condition, memory, and future remain uncertain.

---

## ACT FOUR

### EXT. ROCKS STREET — NIGHT


### INT. ELLA MAE’S HOUSE — LATE NIGHT
Mannie finds Keith’s schoolwork beside bills and a rough cassette. He covers his brother, then sits at the kitchen table until Ella Mae makes him sleep. The family has survived the night; it has not escaped it.

**MANNIE**  
We take the next step.

**KEITH**  
Even when it’s heavy?

**MANNIE**  
Especially then.

---

## ACT FIVE

### EXT. ROCKS BLOCK — DAWN
The neighborhood wakes around the house. Mannie locks the front door, looks at the repaired porch light, and hears the city moving beyond it. No one is rescued by morning. They are only given another choice.

**END OF EPISODE**
