## Episodes 1–5 — Immediate Aftermath

### Episode 1 — Working Title: "Rain on Concrete"
- **Logline:** As sirens approach Ella Mae's house, Manny must keep Keith from fleeing while every friend realizes Bloody Sweet 16 has become a permanent dark square.
- **A Story:** Emergency responders take control; Manny, Ella Mae, Uncle Candyman, and the party witnesses confront the first questions about Chucky.
- **B Story:** Butch gets frightened guests home and Nate preserves the Check Check thread instead of deleting it.
- **Character Focus:** Manny and Keith.
- **Emotional Arc:** Panic hardens into the first acceptance that the night cannot be undone.
- **Key Locations:** Ella Mae's front walk and basement; street in the rain; hospital waiting area.
- **Continuity Notes:** Chucky is unresponsive; do not state a final medical outcome or legal charge.
- **Cliffhanger:** Keith sees Chucky disappear through hospital doors and asks Manny whether he is going to jail.

### Episode 2 — Working Title: "The House Rule"
- **Logline:** Ella Mae's rule about keeping the streets outside collides with the official and emotional aftermath now inside her home.
- **A Story:** Manny and Keith give separate initial accounts; Ella Mae demands honesty without allowing her sons to be consumed by the crowd outside.
- **B Story:** Sarah tries to support Butch and Michelle while recognizing that her own account matters.
- **Character Focus:** Ella Mae, Manny, and Sarah.
- **Emotional Arc:** Safety turns into exposure, then into a fragile family boundary.
- **Key Locations:** Ella Mae's house; precinct/interview room; Sarah's home.
- **Continuity Notes:** Sarah witnessed the late-party sequence; preserve Uncle Candyman as the adult who called 911.
- **Cliffhanger:** A knock at the door signals another request for a statement.

### Episode 3 — Working Title: "Witnesses"
- **Logline:** Competing memories of the fight strain the circle as Nate realizes that being present means carrying responsibility.
- **A Story:** Manny resists shaping Keith's account, while Keith must describe the moment he lost control.
- **B Story:** Nate compares messages and times to help separate rumor from what the group actually saw.
- **Character Focus:** Nutty Nate and Keith.
- **Emotional Arc:** Nate moves from anxious bystander to deliberate witness; Keith moves from denial to shame.
- **Key Locations:** Nate's room; Ella Mae's porch; school hallway.
- **Continuity Notes:** Nate's Check Check messages are established party behavior; they are evidence of tension, not proof of intent.
- **Cliffhanger:** Someone posts a distorted version of the night, and Keith sees it spreading.

### Episode 4 — Working Title: "No Last Song"
- **Logline:** Lucky cannot enter his studio without hearing the final record scratch, while Manny tries to make the room useful again.
- **A Story:** Manny and Lucky face whether music can remain a refuge after the party's violence.
- **B Story:** Butch handles requests for help from people who suddenly expect him to solve everything.
- **Character Focus:** Lucky and Butch.
- **Emotional Arc:** Silence gives way to a cautious decision to keep creating.
- **Key Locations:** Lucky's room/studio; corner outside Lucky's building; Ella Mae's kitchen.
- **Continuity Notes:** Lucky remains the original studio host and keeps the room's danger visible; do not introduce Pretty Ricky yet.
- **Cliffhanger:** Butch receives a message that a familiar street-money contact wants a meeting.

### Episode 5 — Working Title: "After the Banner"
- **Logline:** Manny takes down the crooked birthday banner and realizes protecting Keith now requires more than standing between him and danger.
- **A Story:** The family cleans and repairs the house as Manny chooses to return to school and routine instead of disappearing into panic.
- **B Story:** Butch and Nate help; Sarah and Michelle support Ella Mae; the physical space becomes livable again.
- **Character Focus:** Manny and Ella Mae.
- **Emotional Arc:** Destruction gives way to repair; Manny accepts that protection requires witness, not erasure.
- **Key Locations:** Ella Mae's house; school; neighborhood streets.
- **Continuity Notes:** No legal resolution, medical update, or school consequence is established; the episode closes on family and routine return.
- **Cliffhanger:** Manny's phone buzzes with a message from an unknown contact, suggesting the story's larger world has not forgotten the night.

## Episodes 6–10 — Six Months Later

### Episode 6 — Working Title: "Check Check Returns"
- **Logline:** Social media recirculates the party footage as a new school year begins, and Nate must decide whether to be a witness or a bystander again.
- **A Story:** The thread surfaces; Manny and Keith face renewed scrutiny and rumors.
- **B Story:** Nate recognizes that silence now has a different cost.
- **Character Focus:** Nate and Manny.
- **Emotional Arc:** Observation turns into moral responsibility; silence becomes complicity.
- **Key Locations:** School hallway; Nate's home; social media spaces (text/visual representation only).
- **Continuity Notes:** Do not introduce new characters or legal actors; focus on internal group dynamics and the digital echo of the finale.
- **Cliffhanger:** Nate posts a clarifying thread, and the group's reaction is divided.

### Episode 7 — Working Title: "Lucky's Silence"
- **Logline:** Lucky closes the studio door, telling Manny and Keith that the room is no longer safe and that music cannot carry what happened that night.
- **A Story:** Manny and Lucky face the studio as a crime scene, not a refuge.
- **B Story:** Keith realizes that his studio identity may not survive the aftermath.
- **Character Focus:** Lucky and Keith.
- **Emotional Arc:** Sanctuary becomes contamination; creation turns into guilt.
- **Key Locations:** Lucky's room/studio; Manny's home; hospital or recovery space (unspecified).
- **Continuity Notes:** Lucky's decision is not reversible in this episode; the studio remains closed.
- **Cliffhanger:** Butch receives an offer to use a different space, and Manny must decide whether to accept it.

### Episode 8 — Working Title: "Butch's Offer"
- **Logline:** A street-connected contact offers Butch access to a rival studio and steady money, and Manny must recognize that protection cannot prevent choice.
- **A Story:** Butch considers the offer; Manny tries to talk him down; Sarah is caught between support and alarm.
- **B Story:** The group begins to splinter as the cost of staying close becomes apparent.
- **Character Focus:** Butch and Manny.
- **Emotional Arc:** Loyalty turns into burden; chosen brotherhood is tested by survival pressure.
- **Key Locations:** Street corners; Lucky's building; school; Butch's home.
- **Continuity Notes:** Butch does not accept or reject the offer by episode end; the decision hangs.
- **Cliffhanger:** Butch tells Manny, "I love you, but I can't stay in the aftermath forever."

### Episode 9 — Working Title: "Ella Mae's Discovery"
- **Logline:** Ella Mae finds evidence of Manny's financial stress and his attempt to protect Keith without telling her, forcing a conversation about the limits of protection.
- **A Story:** Ella Mae learns about small money, partnerships, and Manny's decision to risk rather than ask for help.
- **B Story:** Keith overhears and realizes his brother has been sacrificing alone.
- **Character Focus:** Ella Mae and Manny.
- **Emotional Arc:** Protective silence breaks into family honesty; Manny stops hiding the cost.
- **Key Locations:** Ella Mae's house; kitchen conversations; Manny's room.
- **Continuity Notes:** Ella Mae's response is parental and grounded; no supernatural, dramatic, or illegal consequence occurs.
- **Cliffhanger:** Ella Mae tells Manny, "You can't protect Keith if you break yourself," and asks what he needs.

### Episode 10 — Working Title: "Six Months After"
- **Logline:** Manny returns to school and basketball, and realizes that the night will not be undone, but it does not define what happens next.
- **A Story:** Manny attends classes, plays basketball, and begins to carry the night as memory, not identity.
- **B Story:** Keith, Sarah, Butch, and Lucky begin to find their own paths forward, with and without each other.
- **Character Focus:** Manny (ensemble support).
- **Emotional Arc:** Acceptance hardens into resilience; Manny stops waiting for permission to move forward.
- **Key Locations:** School; basketball court; Ella Mae's house; city streets.
- **Continuity Notes:** No major character loss, legal resolution, or music recovery is established; the episode closes on quiet survival.
- **Cliffhanger:** Manny receives a visitor—unspecified, to be approved.

## Episodes 11–15 — One Year Later

### Episode 11 — Working Title: "The Studio Door Opens"
- **Logline:** Lucky reconsiders the studio; the question is not whether to make music again, but how to make music with what happened.
- **A Story:** Lucky invites Manny and Keith back; the room has changed, and so have they.
- **B Story:** The group gathers, and the silence becomes deliberate—a choice to play, not an escape.
- **Character Focus:** Lucky and Manny.
- **Emotional Arc:** Contamination becomes context; the studio survives as a site of witness, not denial.
- **Key Locations:** Lucky's room/studio; surrounding neighborhood; Ella Mae's kitchen.
- **Continuity Notes:** The studio does not become a memorial or a performance stage; it remains a working space.
- **Cliffhanger:** A new artist wants to use the studio, and Lucky must decide whether to bring new people into the room.

### Episode 12 — Working Title: "Butch's Choice"
- **Logline:** Butch chooses to stay or leave, and his decision carries weight for Manny, Sarah, and the circle.
- **A Story:** Butch tells the group of his choice; Sarah's response is clear and patient.
- **B Story:** Manny accepts that chosen brotherhood does not require permanent sameness.
- **Character Focus:** Butch and Sarah.
- **Emotional Arc:** Survival pressure resolves into honesty; the group holds even when paths diverge.
- **Key Locations:** Street corners; Lucky's building; school spaces; Ella Mae's house.
- **Continuity Notes:** If Butch leaves, his exit is grounded and dignified. If he stays, his choice is costly but intentional. Both paths are valid.
- **Cliffhanger:** Butch's choice is announced, and the group resets around it.

### Episode 13 — Working Title: "Keith's Voice"
- **Logline:** Keith begins to freestyle again, and the rhythm is different—slower, more careful, aware of weight.
- **A Story:** Keith practices; Manny listens; Lucky records a first tape.
- **B Story:** Sarah hears Keith's new voice and recognizes the difference.
- **Character Focus:** Keith and Lucky.
- **Emotional Arc:** Spontaneity becomes intentionality; freestyle carries history without drowning in it.
- **Key Locations:** Lucky's studio; school; Manny's home.
- **Continuity Notes:** Keith's first tape is not a triumph or a trauma marker—it is a beginning.
- **Cliffhanger:** The tape circulates among the group, and Nate's response is unexpected.

### Episode 14 — Working Title: "Nate's Silence Breaks"
- **Logline:** Nate finally tells the group what he did not say in the aftermath: the message he deleted, the rumor he did not spread, the choice to protect rather than document.
- **A Story:** Nate's confession; the group's reaction; Manny's understanding and forgiveness.
- **B Story:** Nate recognizes that witness does not require public testimony.
- **Character Focus:** Nate and Manny.
- **Emotional Arc:** Guilt becomes honor; Nate's quiet protection is honored as a form of loyalty.
- **Key Locations:** Group gathering space; Nate's home; Lucky's studio.
- **Continuity Notes:** Nate's deleted message is never revealed; its content is less important than Nate's moral weight.
- **Cliffhanger:** Nate says, "I kept the thing nobody asked me to keep, because I knew you couldn't lose more than you already had."

### Episode 15 — Working Title: "One Year After"
- **Logline:** Manny turns seventeen; the circle gathers again, and the year is marked not by recovery or forgetting, but by survival and intention.
- **A Story:** A small birthday dinner or gathering; Ella Mae is present; the group acknowledges the year.
- **B Story:** Each character reflects on what they have chosen to carry forward.
- **Character Focus:** Manny (ensemble).
- **Emotional Arc:** Time has passed; the night is no longer fresh. What remains is relationship, choice, and the daily work of healing.
- **Key Locations:** Ella Mae's house; Lucky's studio; city spaces.
- **Continuity Notes:** No magical recovery, legal closure, or trauma resolution occurs. The story is still being lived.
- **Cliffhanger:** A new threshold appears—unspecified, to be approved.
