# Dark Squares — Season Two Episode Guide

**Status:** Production planning only. No scenes, dialogue, or screenplay pages are authorized by this document.
**Continuity position:** Starts in the final seconds of Season One Episode 21, *“Bloody Sweet 16,”* and follows the next three years. Season Two has no previously canonized episode titles; titles below are **working titles**, not established canon.

## Episodes 1–5 — Immediate Aftermath

### Episode 1 — Working Title: “Rain on Concrete”
- **Logline:** As sirens approach Ella Mae’s house, Manny must keep Keith from fleeing while every friend realizes Bloody Sweet 16 has become a permanent dark square.
- **A Story:** Emergency responders take control; Manny, Ella Mae, Uncle Candyman, and the party witnesses confront the first questions about Chucky.
- **B Story:** Butch gets frightened guests home and Nate preserves the Check Check thread instead of deleting it.
- **Character Focus:** Manny and Keith.
- **Emotional Arc:** Panic hardens into the first acceptance that the night cannot be undone.
- **Key Locations:** Ella Mae’s front walk and basement; street in the rain; hospital waiting area.
- **Continuity Notes:** Chucky is unresponsive; do not state a final medical outcome or legal charge.
- **Cliffhanger:** Keith sees Chucky disappear through hospital doors and asks Manny whether he is going to jail.

### Episode 2 — Working Title: “The House Rule”
- **Logline:** Ella Mae’s rule about keeping the streets outside collides with the official and emotional aftermath now inside her home.
- **A Story:** Manny and Keith give separate initial accounts; Ella Mae demands honesty without allowing her sons to be consumed by the crowd outside.
- **B Story:** Sarah tries to support Butch and Michelle while recognizing that her own account matters.
- **Character Focus:** Ella Mae, Manny, and Sarah.
- **Emotional Arc:** Safety turns into exposure, then into a fragile family boundary.
- **Key Locations:** Ella Mae’s house; precinct/interview room; Sarah’s home.
- **Continuity Notes:** Sarah witnessed the late-party sequence; preserve Uncle Candyman as the adult who called 911.
- **Cliffhanger:** A knock at the door signals another request for a statement.

### Episode 3 — Working Title: “Witnesses”
- **Logline:** Competing memories of the fight strain the circle as Nate realizes that being present means carrying responsibility.
- **A Story:** Manny resists shaping Keith’s account, while Keith must describe the moment he lost control.
- **B Story:** Nate compares messages and times to help separate rumor from what the group actually saw.
- **Character Focus:** Nutty Nate and Keith.
- **Emotional Arc:** Nate moves from anxious bystander to deliberate witness; Keith moves from denial to shame.
- **Key Locations:** Nate’s room; Ella Mae’s porch; school hallway.
- **Continuity Notes:** Nate’s Check Check messages are established party behavior; they are evidence of tension, not proof of intent.
- **Cliffhanger:** Someone posts a distorted version of the night, and Keith sees it spreading.

### Episode 4 — Working Title: “No Last Song”
- **Logline:** Lucky cannot enter his studio without hearing the final record scratch, while Manny tries to make the room useful again.
- **A Story:** Manny and Lucky face whether music can remain a refuge after the party’s violence.
- **B Story:** Butch handles requests for help from people who suddenly expect him to solve everything.
- **Character Focus:** Lucky and Butch.
- **Emotional Arc:** Silence gives way to a cautious decision to keep creating.
- **Key Locations:** Lucky’s room/studio; corner outside Lucky’s building; Ella Mae’s kitchen.
- **Continuity Notes:** Lucky remains the original studio host and keeps the room’s danger visible; do not introduce Pretty Ricky yet.
- **Cliffhanger:** Butch receives a message that a familiar street-money contact wants a meeting.

### Episode 5 — Working Title: “After the Banner”
- **Logline:** Manny takes down the crooked birthday banner and realizes protecting Keith now requires more than standing between him and danger.
- **A Story:** The family cleans and repairs the house as Manny chooses to return to school and routine instead of disappearing into panic.
- **B Story:** Sarah checks in on Michelle and discovers how quickly fear is isolating the people who were at the party.
- **Character Focus:** Manny and Sarah.
- **Emotional Arc:** Grief becomes work; care becomes a choice rather than a feeling.
- **Key Locations:** Ella Mae’s basement and yard; high school; bus stop.
- **Continuity Notes:** Preserve Sarah/Manny’s quiet history without creating a romance or betraying Butch/Sarah.
- **Cliffhanger:** Manny finds Keith awake with headphones on, unable to press play.

## Episodes 6–10 — The Months That Follow

### Episode 6 — Working Title: “Static”
- **Logline:** Rumor turns every ordinary trip through the Woods and Rocks into a test of who still belongs to the circle.
- **A Story:** Keith returns to school under scrutiny; Manny cannot fight every comment without making things worse.
- **B Story:** Butch starts organizing rides and check-ins, gaining followers he never asked for.
- **Character Focus:** Keith and Butch.
- **Emotional Arc:** Keith’s isolation meets Butch’s reluctant authority.
- **Key Locations:** High school; Woods corner; Rocks driveway.
- **Continuity Notes:** The neighborhoods retain distinct social rules; no city is named.
- **Cliffhanger:** A stranger asks Butch whether he is “the one running things now.”

### Episode 7 — Working Title: “Angles”
- **Logline:** Manny uses the discipline basketball taught him to redirect a brewing confrontation, but learns the group expects him to make every call.
- **A Story:** A neighborhood challenge threatens to pull Keith back into public conflict; Manny finds a nonviolent exit.
- **B Story:** Lucky lets Keith sit in during a private session, but Keith cannot yet use his voice.
- **Character Focus:** Manny and Keith.
- **Emotional Arc:** Manny feels competent, then trapped by being needed.
- **Key Locations:** Garage court; Lucky’s studio; alley near the Rocks.
- **Continuity Notes:** Manny’s basketball style and protective role remain central.
- **Cliffhanger:** Keith finally records one raw line, then deletes it.

### Episode 8 — Working Title: “Small Money”
- **Logline:** Butch tries to close off the small-money activity that once linked him and Manny, only to find old obligations resist clean exits.
- **A Story:** Manny and Butch disagree over what they owe others and whether stepping back looks like weakness.
- **B Story:** Nate quietly builds a contact list so nobody has to rely on unsafe rides or vague messages.
- **Character Focus:** Butch and Nutty Nate.
- **Emotional Arc:** Butch’s confidence becomes caution; Nate finds purpose in preparation.
- **Key Locations:** Coney Island; Nate’s room; bodega exterior.
- **Continuity Notes:** Keep the activity non-specific; it remains a source of risk, not a how-to plot.
- **Cliffhanger:** Butch receives notice that he must appear for questioning.

### Episode 9 — Working Title: “The Long Walk”
- **Logline:** Manny accompanies Butch through a day of legal uncertainty and sees how leadership can turn friends into liabilities.
- **A Story:** Butch consults counsel and prepares for questioning without knowing who is saying what about the party and old business.
- **B Story:** Sarah and Keith share an awkward, honest conversation about being watched by everyone.
- **Character Focus:** Butch, Sarah, and Keith.
- **Emotional Arc:** Butch confronts fear beneath his calm; Keith recognizes he is not the only one carrying a name.
- **Key Locations:** Legal office; transit route; Sarah’s porch.
- **Continuity Notes:** Do not specify an offense, case outcome, jurisdiction, or charge.
- **Cliffhanger:** Butch is told to return with records he does not know how to explain.

### Episode 10 — Working Title: “A Room with a Door”
- **Logline:** Lucky turns a studio rule into a promise of safety, and Manny begins to believe structure can protect the group better than secrecy.
- **A Story:** Lucky institutes clear studio boundaries; Manny backs him even when Keith feels singled out.
- **B Story:** Nate notices someone has been tracking when Sarah leaves school.
- **Character Focus:** Lucky, Manny, and Nate.
- **Emotional Arc:** The group trades easy access for earned trust.
- **Key Locations:** Lucky’s studio; high school gate; Ella Mae’s porch.
- **Continuity Notes:** This is vigilance, not proof of an attacker’s identity.
- **Cliffhanger:** Sarah finds an unsigned warning tucked into her school materials.

## Episodes 11–15 — Year One: Sarah’s Attack

### Episode 11 — Working Title: “Check Check”
- **Logline:** Nate’s habit of checking every detail becomes the group’s first real safety system as threats around Sarah grow harder to dismiss.
- **A Story:** Nate coordinates rides and check-ins; Manny must decide whether to tell Ella Mae how much risk is near them.
- **B Story:** Butch’s increasing obligations pull him away from Sarah when she needs steadiness.
- **Character Focus:** Nutty Nate and Sarah.
- **Emotional Arc:** Sarah moves from irritation at caution to naming what scares her; Nate learns care can be leadership.
- **Key Locations:** High school; Nate’s room; Ella Mae’s kitchen.
- **Continuity Notes:** Sarah retains choice about who knows what and how she travels.
- **Cliffhanger:** Sarah misses a scheduled check-in.

### Episode 12 — Working Title: “The Call Back”
- **Logline:** The friends search for Sarah without turning panic into retaliation, while Butch’s need to act threatens the fragile plan.
- **A Story:** Manny and Nate coordinate a safe, adult-supported search response; Butch pursues a lead alone.
- **B Story:** Keith returns to the microphone to record a message for Sarah, then cannot send it.
- **Character Focus:** Butch, Manny, and Keith.
- **Emotional Arc:** Urgency tests whether the circle can choose discipline over revenge.
- **Key Locations:** Streets between school and home; Lucky’s studio; hospital exterior.
- **Continuity Notes:** Do not depict an attack before it occurs; avoid vigilante framing.
- **Cliffhanger:** Nate’s phone rings from Sarah’s number, but no one speaks.

### Episode 13 — Working Title: “Hold the Line”
- **Logline:** As the search narrows, Butch is pulled into another legal encounter that makes his help both necessary and suspect.
- **A Story:** Butch must answer legal questions while trying to keep Sarah’s situation from becoming a public spectacle.
- **B Story:** Ella Mae insists Manny bring adults into the response, challenging his instinct to carry it alone.
- **Character Focus:** Butch and Ella Mae.
- **Emotional Arc:** Butch learns control is not the same as protection; Manny accepts help.
- **Key Locations:** Legal office; Ella Mae’s house; transit platform.
- **Continuity Notes:** Butch’s legal problems escalate but remain procedurally unspecified.
- **Cliffhanger:** Sarah is found alive, injured, and asking for space.

### Episode 14 — Working Title: “Sarah”
- **Logline:** After an attack, Sarah defines the terms of her own safety while the people who love her struggle not to turn her pain into their agenda.
- **A Story:** Sarah receives care and gives a limited account on her own terms; Butch must listen rather than take control.
- **B Story:** Manny keeps Keith from chasing rumors, using the restraint he once wished Keith had shown.
- **Character Focus:** Sarah and Butch.
- **Emotional Arc:** Sarah moves from shock to the first assertion of agency; Butch moves from rage to presence.
- **Key Locations:** Hospital; Sarah’s home; Ella Mae’s kitchen.
- **Continuity Notes:** The attack is a permanent milestone; do not identify an assailant or resolve a case without later approval.
- **Cliffhanger:** Sarah tells Butch she needs him to stop making choices for her.

### Episode 15 — Working Title: “Not a Rumor”
- **Logline:** The circle confronts the stories people tell about Sarah’s attack, and Sarah chooses which parts of her life she will reclaim.
- **A Story:** Sarah returns briefly to a familiar place with boundaries; Manny and Nate enforce them without speaking for her.
- **B Story:** Pretty Ricky hears Li Keith’s unfinished recording and asks Lucky for an introduction.
- **Character Focus:** Sarah, Nate, and Keith.
- **Emotional Arc:** Isolation becomes a cautious reconnection; Keith hears a possible future in someone else’s belief.
- **Key Locations:** Sarah’s porch; Lucky’s studio; school courtyard.
- **Continuity Notes:** Pretty Ricky is only set up here; his formal mentorship begins next block. Li Keith is Keith’s artist identity.
- **Cliffhanger:** Pretty Ricky leaves word: “If he’s serious, let him show up on time.”

## Episodes 16–20 — Year Two: Discipline and Pressure

### Episode 16 — Working Title: “On Time”
- **Logline:** Pretty Ricky tests Manny and Li Keith with the unglamorous discipline behind music, while Butch’s leadership is challenged by an old associate.
- **A Story:** Manny and Keith meet Pretty Ricky and learn that rehearsal, preparation, and ownership matter as much as talent.
- **B Story:** Butch refuses a request that would pull the group back toward risk.
- **Character Focus:** Pretty Ricky, Manny, and Li Keith.
- **Emotional Arc:** Hope becomes responsibility; Butch discovers saying no can cost him standing.
- **Key Locations:** Pretty Ricky’s rehearsal space; Lucky’s studio; neighborhood corner.
- **Continuity Notes:** Pretty Ricky is a local mentor, not a replacement for Lucky or a bridge to New York/Wizard.
- **Cliffhanger:** An old contact tells Butch refusal does not erase a debt.

### Episode 17 — Working Title: “The Meter”
- **Logline:** Pretty Ricky makes Li Keith perform without hiding behind the crowd, and Manny must stop rescuing his brother mid-failure.
- **A Story:** Li Keith struggles through a small rehearsal but returns to finish it; Manny learns to offer craft instead of control.
- **B Story:** Nate traces a pattern in the legal requests surrounding Butch.
- **Character Focus:** Li Keith, Manny, and Nate.
- **Emotional Arc:** Keith converts shame into work; Manny practices letting him own the result.
- **Key Locations:** Rehearsal room; Manny’s garage; Nate’s room.
- **Continuity Notes:** Keith’s musical growth is local and incremental; no record deal or national attention.
- **Cliffhanger:** Nate finds a date that puts Butch’s hearing on the same day as the group’s first showcase.

### Episode 18 — Working Title: “Lead”
- **Logline:** Butch’s effort to shield everyone from his legal pressure isolates him from Sarah and makes Manny question who is really leading.
- **A Story:** Butch skips a crucial planning meeting to handle legal obligations, then refuses to explain himself.
- **B Story:** Sarah sets firm terms for attending music events, and the group learns to follow her lead.
- **Character Focus:** Butch and Sarah.
- **Emotional Arc:** Butch’s stoicism becomes loneliness; Sarah’s boundaries become a model for the circle.
- **Key Locations:** Courthouse exterior; Sarah’s home; rehearsal space.
- **Continuity Notes:** Keep legal proceedings non-specific and avoid a verdict.
- **Cliffhanger:** Butch receives a condition that could keep him away from people he wants to protect.

### Episode 19 — Working Title: “Home Work”
- **Logline:** Manny’s responsibilities at Ella Mae’s house force him to choose between a needed rehearsal and an immediate family obligation.
- **A Story:** Manny manages repairs, bills, and Keith’s needs after Ella Mae’s long shift; he misses rehearsal but prevents a home crisis.
- **B Story:** Lucky and Pretty Ricky disagree about how much pressure Li Keith can handle.
- **Character Focus:** Manny, Ella Mae, Lucky, and Pretty Ricky.
- **Emotional Arc:** Manny stops treating caretaking as an interruption and begins treating it as a choice with costs.
- **Key Locations:** Ella Mae’s house; Lucky’s studio; Pretty Ricky’s space.
- **Continuity Notes:** Ella Mae remains the guardian; Manny has increasing responsibility, not parental replacement.
- **Cliffhanger:** Li Keith tells Manny the showcase cannot wait for his schedule.

### Episode 20 — Working Title: “First Take”
- **Logline:** Li Keith records a complete track for the first time, but the achievement is shadowed by a legal development that leaves Butch absent.
- **A Story:** With Lucky engineering and Pretty Ricky directing, Li Keith completes a truthful local demo; Manny supports without taking over.
- **B Story:** Nate learns why Butch cannot make the session and chooses what the group needs to know.
- **Character Focus:** Li Keith, Lucky, and Nutty Nate.
- **Emotional Arc:** A voice returns; celebration is tempered by loyalty and uncertainty.
- **Key Locations:** Lucky’s studio; Pretty Ricky’s rehearsal room; Nate’s room.
- **Continuity Notes:** Lucky’s technical role stays essential. No named label, industry deal, New York, or Wizard material.
- **Cliffhanger:** Butch calls from outside a legal meeting: “Don’t cancel it. Just be careful.”

## Episodes 21–25 — Early Year Three: Tests of the New Path

### Episode 21 — Working Title: “Receipts”
- **Logline:** A paperwork deadline turns Butch’s informal leadership into a burden, while Nate’s records prove that preparation can protect people.
- **A Story:** Butch needs documentation and trustworthy witnesses; Manny must decide how much involvement helps versus harms.
- **B Story:** Nate builds a simple operating plan for rides, sessions, and payments around the music work.
- **Character Focus:** Butch and Nutty Nate.
- **Emotional Arc:** Butch accepts help; Nate recognizes his systems have value.
- **Key Locations:** Nate’s room; legal office; studio.
- **Continuity Notes:** No specific legal theory, result, or charge is established.
- **Cliffhanger:** A missed requirement threatens to derail Butch’s next appearance.

### Episode 22 — Working Title: “The Price of a Verse”
- **Logline:** Li Keith is offered quick local exposure on terms that conflict with Pretty Ricky’s standards and Manny’s hard-won caution.
- **A Story:** Keith must choose whether to perform where the crowd is volatile; Pretty Ricky insists a career starts with what an artist refuses.
- **B Story:** Sarah considers attending the showcase rehearsal and sets a safety plan with Nate.
- **Character Focus:** Li Keith, Pretty Ricky, and Sarah.
- **Emotional Arc:** Temptation yields to self-respect; Sarah chooses participation, not disappearance.
- **Key Locations:** Rehearsal space; local venue exterior; Sarah’s home.
- **Continuity Notes:** The opportunity remains local; do not seed a later New York pipeline.
- **Cliffhanger:** Keith walks away from the offer and sees the promoter turn his attention to someone else.

### Episode 23 — Working Title: “Fault Lines”
- **Logline:** Manny’s attempt to keep home, court obligations, and music separate fails, forcing the brothers to name what they still blame each other for.
- **A Story:** Manny and Keith argue over responsibility for a missed obligation, finally connecting current pressure to the night with Chucky.
- **B Story:** Butch and Sarah attempt an honest conversation without demanding forgiveness or certainty.
- **Character Focus:** Manny, Keith, Butch, and Sarah.
- **Emotional Arc:** Resentment becomes truth; truth creates room for a less controlling brotherhood.
- **Key Locations:** Ella Mae’s kitchen; porch; courthouse exterior.
- **Continuity Notes:** Keith’s remorse remains ongoing; do not declare Chucky’s outcome.
- **Cliffhanger:** Ella Mae finds Manny asleep at the kitchen table beside overdue notices and rehearsal notes.

### Episode 24 — Working Title: “Open Door”
- **Logline:** Manny asks Ella Mae for help instead of hiding the strain, while Pretty Ricky turns the group’s rehearsal into a test of community.
- **A Story:** Ella Mae and Manny renegotiate responsibilities at home; Manny delegates rather than carrying every task alone.
- **B Story:** Pretty Ricky makes Li Keith perform for a small trusted room, including Sarah only if she chooses to attend.
- **Character Focus:** Manny, Ella Mae, and Li Keith.
- **Emotional Arc:** Manny exchanges shame for partnership; Li Keith learns presence is earned.
- **Key Locations:** Ella Mae’s house; Pretty Ricky’s rehearsal space; Lucky’s studio.
- **Continuity Notes:** Sarah’s attendance is her decision. Keep the home as a boundary that requires active repair.
- **Cliffhanger:** The trusted-room performance is interrupted by news that Butch’s legal situation has escalated.

### Episode 25 — Working Title: “No Shortcuts”
- **Logline:** With Butch under sharper pressure, the group rejects a risky shortcut to finance its showcase and discovers that restraint has a price.
- **A Story:** Manny, Nate, and Li Keith choose a smaller, slower event rather than money tied to unsafe expectations.
- **B Story:** Butch confronts the damage his secrecy caused and asks Sarah what support actually looks like.
- **Character Focus:** Manny, Butch, and Sarah.
- **Emotional Arc:** The group turns down immediate relief to preserve a future; Butch begins to listen.
- **Key Locations:** Studio; local venue; Sarah’s porch.
- **Continuity Notes:** Do not depict or operationalize criminal activity; the choice is moral and practical.
- **Cliffhanger:** The venue owner offers the group one date—on the third anniversary of Bloody Sweet 16.

## Episodes 26–30 — Third Anniversary: A Limited Way Forward

### Episode 26 — Working Title: “The Date”
- **Logline:** The anniversary showcase makes each friend decide whether memory should stop the future or make them approach it differently.
- **A Story:** Manny considers canceling; Li Keith insists the date can be marked without being exploited.
- **B Story:** Sarah tells Butch she will attend only if the event is built around safety rather than protection theater.
- **Character Focus:** Manny, Li Keith, and Sarah.
- **Emotional Arc:** Dread becomes a plan rooted in consent and care.
- **Key Locations:** Ella Mae’s house; venue; rehearsal space.
- **Continuity Notes:** The anniversary is of the party/fight; Chucky’s ultimate status remains unspoken and unresolved.
- **Cliffhanger:** Nate reports an unfamiliar person has been asking about the event.

### Episode 27 — Working Title: “Keep Watch”
- **Logline:** Nate coordinates a safety plan for the showcase without letting fear turn the night into another closed circle.
- **A Story:** Nate, Manny, Lucky, and Pretty Ricky create clear roles, exits, rides, and adult support; Sarah approves only the parts that involve her.
- **B Story:** Butch must meet a legal obligation that may make him late or absent.
- **Character Focus:** Nutty Nate, Sarah, and Butch.
- **Emotional Arc:** Nate claims earned authority; Butch faces the pain of not controlling the room.
- **Key Locations:** Venue; Nate’s room; legal office.
- **Continuity Notes:** Safety is collaborative and non-vigilante. Uncle Candyman may advise as a trusted adult.
- **Cliffhanger:** A technical failure threatens to cancel Li Keith’s set.

### Episode 28 — Working Title: “Soundcheck”
- **Logline:** When equipment fails and old tensions flare, Manny has to lead by trusting the people he once tried to manage.
- **A Story:** Lucky and Pretty Ricky troubleshoot; Manny assigns decisions instead of grabbing them, and Li Keith adapts his performance.
- **B Story:** Sarah chooses to arrive with support, not because fear has vanished but because she refuses to be erased.
- **Character Focus:** Manny, Lucky, Pretty Ricky, and Sarah.
- **Emotional Arc:** Control loosens into collaboration; fear coexists with agency.
- **Key Locations:** Venue stage and back room; Sarah’s home; Ella Mae’s porch.
- **Continuity Notes:** Lucky’s technical competence and Pretty Ricky’s mentorship complement each other.
- **Cliffhanger:** The crowd begins arriving as word reaches Manny that Butch has been delayed.

### Episode 29 — Working Title: “Three Years”
- **Logline:** On the third anniversary of Bloody Sweet 16, Li Keith takes the stage while the circle confronts who they were, what they lost, and what they refuse to repeat.
- **A Story:** Li Keith performs a local set shaped by the season’s work; Manny supports from offstage and lets Keith own the moment.
- **B Story:** Sarah and Butch face the event together on Sarah’s terms; Nate keeps the room steady without making himself the story.
- **Character Focus:** Li Keith, Manny, Sarah, Butch, and Nate.
- **Emotional Arc:** Memory is acknowledged rather than conquered; community becomes a deliberate practice.
- **Key Locations:** Local venue; backstage; street outside.
- **Continuity Notes:** This is a modest local milestone, not an industry launch. No New York/Wizard/later-series introduction.
- **Cliffhanger:** After the set, Manny receives word that demands an immediate family decision.

### Episode 30 — Working Title: “The Next Square”
- **Logline:** Manny chooses an accountable path for home and family as the circle accepts that moving forward means carrying consequence together, not escaping it.
- **A Story:** Manny makes the hard family decision prompted by Episode 29 and openly shares responsibility with Ella Mae and Keith rather than hiding it.
- **B Story:** Butch faces the next legal step with support; Sarah defines her continued place in the circle; Pretty Ricky gives Li Keith a disciplined next assignment.
- **Character Focus:** Manny, Ella Mae, Keith, Butch, Sarah, Nutty Nate, Pretty Ricky, and Lucky.
- **Emotional Arc:** The season closes on sober hope: no one is absolved, but no one has to be abandoned.
- **Key Locations:** Ella Mae’s house; Lucky’s studio; Pretty Ricky’s rehearsal space; venue exterior at dawn.
- **Continuity Notes:** End within the local three-year period. Preserve open legal and Chucky-outcome questions; do not open later-series New York or Wizard material.
- **Cliffhanger:** Li Keith steps to the microphone for the next rehearsal as Manny watches the house lights come on—an earned forward motion, not a resolved ending.
