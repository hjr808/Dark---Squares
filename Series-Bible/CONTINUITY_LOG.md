## Locked Season Two Planning Decisions

| Item | Decision | Scope / guardrail |
|---|---|---|
| Season Two duration | Season Two starts directly after the rain-soaked ending of Season One Episode 21, *“Bloody Sweet 16,”* and covers the first three years after the Sweet Sixteen era. | No unexplained jump past this period. |
| Chucky aftermath | The immediate emergency, witness response, and legal/social consequences are dramatized, but Chucky’s ultimate medical outcome and any specific legal disposition remain unconfirmed. | Do not state death, survival, charge, verdict, or sentence as canon without approval. |

| Butch | Butch grows into a practical leader and faces escalating legal pressure tied to the group’s environment and his associations. | No charge, jurisdiction, plea, verdict, or sentence is locked. Preserve Butch/Sarah’s established relationship. |

| Manny’s responsibility | Manny increasingly manages family, home, music, and difficult moral choices while learning that protection cannot become control. | Use **Manny** in detailed-manuscript-continuous material; “Mannie” remains an unresolved alternate spelling. |
| Later-series firewall | New York, Wizard, and other later-series storylines are not introduced in Season Two. | No relocation, named New York connection, Wizard appearance/reference, or later-series payoff before its approved timeline. |

## Timeline Milestones

1. **Season One endpoint:** Chucky is unresponsive after Keith slams him onto concrete outside Ella Mae’s house; Uncle Candyman calls 911.
2. **Season Two opening:** Emergency services, family response, witness accounts, and the breach in the group’s sense of safety begin immediately.
3. **Season Two Year One:** The circle lives under scrutiny; Sarah’s attack makes the stakes of safety, care, and retaliation unavoidable.
4. **Season Two Year Two:** Pretty Ricky’s mentorship and Li Keith’s serious music work create a structured alternative while Butch’s legal pressure grows.
5. **Season Two Year Three:** Manny chooses accountable stewardship over secrecy/control; the circle reaches a modest local music milestone at the third-anniversary threshold.
