# Dark Squares — Continuity Log

## Season Two, Episodes 6–10

- **Time:** These episodes occur three years after Bloody Sweet 16. Mannie is nineteen and Li Keith (formerly Keith in childhood material) is fifteen.
- **Chucky:** Survived the Sweet 16 injury, uses a cane, and has ongoing medical needs. His guardian brings a civil action; a confidential settlement establishes a medical fund. Chucky does not forgive Li Keith and no reconciliation is claimed.
- **Sarah:** Was assaulted before Episode 6 by Marlon Vee, an acquaintance previously questioned by police. She is in trauma counseling and leads/attends a community-center survivor circle. She identifies a threatening voicemail; Marlon stalks the center and is arrested in Episode 10 on stalking and voicemail evidence, with further charges unresolved. Sarah sets her own safety boundaries and is not treated as a passive rescue object.
- **Butch:** Is subject to deposition/civil-case pressure because of his Sweet 16 security role and has an old municipal-citation issue. He responds through counsel (Ms. Vale), gives evidence lawfully, avoids vigilantism, and begins continuing community-center safety work. His leadership becomes consent-based and accountable.
- **Mannie:** Receives a conditional basketball-development opportunity, is temporarily suspended over an incomplete old-party record, and is reinstated on attendance and a youth-designed community-accountability plan. He remains producer, brother, and coach; he no longer promises to be everywhere.
- **Li Keith:** Uses the professional name Li Keith. His demo is posted and sampled without permission by promoter Drew. With Lucky, he protects files, disputes the theft, performs “Open File,” and signs a paid supervised development slot that preserves his ownership and credits Mannie and Lucky.
- **Locations:** The community center becomes Sarah’s recovery and safety-work site. Ella Mae approves the garage as a supervised music-and-basketball workshop.
- **Unresolved:** A courier delivers Mannie a Sweet 16 photograph marked “THE RECORD ISN’T CLOSED.” The sender and purpose are unknown. Marlon’s final criminal case remains unresolved. Mannie’s civil-case and accountability obligations continue.
