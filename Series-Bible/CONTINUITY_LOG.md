# Dark Squares — Continuity Log

## Season Two, Episodes 1–5

> This log records the permanent story developments established by the Season Two screenplays. “Mannie” is used in these production-facing scripts; it refers to the character rendered “Manny” in the existing detailed Season One manuscript.

| Timeline | Event / status | Continuity impact |
|---|---|---|
| Bloody Sweet 16, night of | Keith slams Chucky onto concrete after their rain-soaked fight. Chucky survives the initial emergency but is critically injured. | The event is publicly called **Bloody Sweet 16**; police open an investigation. Keith’s responsibility and remorse remain central. |
| First day after | Ella Mae’s house is treated as a compromised refuge after police and an ambulance arrive. | Ella Mae strengthens the house rule: no drama, retaliation, or street business is permitted at her home. |
| First three months | Chucky awakens with incomplete memory and begins a prolonged recovery. | His medical and legal future remains unresolved; no charge has been filed during this period. Keith offers apologies without receiving forgiveness. |
| First three months | Keith records as **Li Keith** and begins developing songs with Lucky, Cheerio, Nate, Sarah, and Mannie. | Li Keith is Keith’s public music name, not a separate character. His music must be accountable and must not exploit Chucky’s injury. |
| First year | Sarah is attacked in a school service alley by an unidentified hooded assailant and escapes with bruising after fighting free. | Sarah gives a police statement. A cassette with rain, breathing, and a clicking pendant is left at her home; the attacker’s identity remains unproved. |
| First year | Sarah and Butch explicitly agree that his role is to listen and walk beside her, not retaliate for her. | Their romantic connection continues, with Sarah retaining agency over the investigation and her safety. |
| Years one–two | Butch becomes an informal neighborhood mediator and organizes safe walks. | His influence rises because people trust his calm judgment. He rejects Boy George’s invitation to turn that influence into paid street control. |
| Two years | A runner associated with Boy George is linked to the cassette by a pendant, limp, and borrowed studio tape, but is found beaten and does not cooperate. | The evidence is a lead, not proof. Boy George’s role in Sarah’s attack remains unresolved. |
| Two years | Mannie secretly takes deliveries to help with household money, then quits. | Mannie and Butch permanently end their small street-money partnership. Mannie’s responsibilities increase, but Ella Mae insists he is not her replacement. |
| Two years | Li Keith is offered a supervised youth showcase after a local DJ hears a clean mix. | Li Keith performs publicly for the first time, beginning a local music-career path with Lucky’s guidance. |
| Three years | Boy George is picked up on an unrelated warrant. | His pending status does not resolve the Sarah attack investigation or make him legally responsible for it. |
| Three years | Butch establishes a weekly open basketball court rather than taking over Boy George’s role. | Butch’s influence is redirected toward community protection and mentorship, while its future cost remains an open concern. |
| Three years | Chucky requires rehabilitation; he tells Keith he is still here and wants his story told accurately. | Keith and Chucky’s relationship is not repaired, but both acknowledge the ongoing consequence. |
| Three years | Li Keith receives a supervised studio-session opportunity and Mannie agrees to support the visit with Ella Mae involved. | The music career advances locally only. No New York, Wizard, or later-series storyline has begun. |

## Continuing Unresolved Storylines

- Chucky’s eventual medical recovery, memory, investigation outcome, and legal consequences for Keith.
- The identity and motive of Sarah’s attacker, including the extent of Boy George’s involvement.
- Whether Butch can retain community influence without being drawn into the power structure he rejects.
- Whether Mannie can balance school, family care, and Li Keith’s growing opportunities without taking on adult burdens that belong to Ella Mae.
