## 2. Master Chronology: Apartment Years, Woods Years, and the Move

### A. Apartment years / Woods childhood — Episodes 1–6

1. **The Woods** establishes Manny and Keith in Ella Mae's low four-family apartment, near the basement hall, laundry, and back entrance. Ella Mae's governing rule is that drama and the streets do not enter her house.
2. Manny, four years older than Keith, starts carrying father-like responsibility while Ella Mae works late. Kizzy is the brothers' protective little dog.
3. The Woods supplies games, bikes, porches, alleys, empty lots, boom boxes, courts, the junkyard, and the graveyard. It is both childhood world and a place requiring vigilance.
4. The apartment-door test in **Monday** confirms the home boundary: Manny sends boys away, hides the bat when Ella Mae arrives, and the brothers avoid allowing conflict into the apartment.
5. In **Junkyard Kings**, Manny and Keith create private brotherhood away from crowds; Manny taps a steering-wheel rhythm and Keith raps over it.
6. In **The Graveyard Bet**, the brothers' nighttime dare becomes a comic shared memory; Kizzy leads the escape.
7. In **Steve's Turntables**, Steve teaches Manny blending; Keith discovers Manny's practice tape and freestyles.
8. In **Night Lights**, Ella Mae's late work shifts create borrowed nighttime freedom. Kickball becomes stickball, basketball, and a bike race; Manny accepts Keith's “One more ride.”

### B. Move to the Rocks — Episode 7

1. Ella Mae announces: **“We found a house.”**
2. The family leaves the apartment/Woods for a Rocks house with a basement, garage, fence, yard, porch, and front door belonging to the family.
3. The move is material progress, not a moral or social reset. The Woods' memories, reputation, and lessons remain active.
4. Keith notices the new block watching. Manny answers with the Woods identity rather than a claim to rule the Rocks.

### C. Rocks years / adolescent expansion — Episodes 8–16

1. **The Test:** Rocks boys challenge the brothers; Manny establishes that they are not easy prey through restraint during basketball, not conquest.
2. **Butch:** Manny meets Butch through driveway basketball. Mutual respect begins the friendship.
3. **Lucky's Room:** Steve brings Manny to Lucky's makeshift studio, where music knowledge and danger coexist.
4. **Dope Fein Dinner:** Manny and Butch begin small money activity; access, jokes, and innocence change.
5. **The Max Julian / Coney Nights:** Manny meets Nate after the winter robbery; Nate later joins the core circle at Coney.
6. **First Day / Sarah:** High school expands the group. Sarah meets Butch first, then joins the crew.
7. **The Fallen:** Fall imagery tracks the partnership, fading daylight, and thinning innocence.

### D. Sweet Sixteen night / Season One finale — Episodes 17–21

1. The invitation spreads by word of mouth through Woods, Rocks, school, and friendship circles.
2. Ella Mae entrusts Manny with her house; Uncle Candyman oversees while she works.
3. The party starts with basement food/gifts and backyard music, becomes an after-party, and appears to end safely.
4. The final confrontation moves from backyard to the front of Ella Mae's house in rain.
5. Chucky becomes unresponsive after Keith slams him onto concrete; this is the final established event.
